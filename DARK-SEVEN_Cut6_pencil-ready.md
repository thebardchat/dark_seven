# DARK SEVEN — CUT 6
## Pencil-Ready Script · *The Knife That Stays*
### the last cut · stabbed · bleeding out · the floor drops all the way · the boy that made the man
*Picks up off the held white at the end of Cut 5. Color law absolute: BLACK = the world/the dark · RED = blood, the only hue · WHITE = grace, earned and rare. Veil holds: the phantom is a hand and a warm light, never a face; Keystone appears only as a silhouette, safe behind the shield. `(RED)` = the one color on the panel. `(WHITE)` = an earned white-out. The Devil does not win.*

> **NOTE.** Cut 6 is the payload of the wound map. It carries the reveal (the knife has been in his back since 1996) and the thesis (the wound made the wall). The full straight-through origin it drops into is scripted separately — see **THE FLOOR / '96, THE ORIGIN** in the master adaptation (pp. 44–56), told slow where this tells it as a plunge.

## PAGE N — THE LAST PATIENT CUT
*Come out of Cut 5's white. 4 panels. The stab that doesn't leave.*

**PANEL 1.** Bleed down out of the held **(WHITE)** of Cut 5 into grey. The man barely on his feet, shield still up, four wounds bleeding **(RED)**. Far edge of the frame: **the hand.** It has waited five cuts. Now its off-panel warm light *shifts* — the first time it has moved in the whole book.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A thing that patient doesn't leave because a shield went up.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): It waits for the arm holding the shield to be the arm already cut in four places.

**PANEL 2.** EXTREME TIGHT: the shield-arm trembling — dropping a hair. The exact tired inch.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(steady, no drip now)* There. That inch. That's all I ever needed.

**PANEL 3.** It doesn't slash. It **STABS.** Low in the back. Draw the blade going *in* — and unlike every blade before it, no spray, no drawn line. It goes to the handle and stops.
> SFX: **thunk** *(small, final, wet — the opposite of every big SFX before it)*

**PANEL 4.** The blade **STAYS.** Buried to the handle. No motion out. The **(RED)** comes up around it slow and total — not sheeting. Welling.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Every other blade in this book drew its line and pulled away.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): This one has no intention of leaving.

## PAGE O — BLEEDING OUT, FOR REAL
*3 panels. The floor coming up.*

**PANEL 1.** WIDE, high angle looking down: he goes down onto one knee in the grey. Shield still up in a hand that's forgetting why. The **(RED)** pooling under him.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Bleeding out. For real this time. The floor coming up.

**PANEL 2.** The shield lowering by degrees. His grip loosening finger by finger.

**PANEL 3.** His free hand reaches *back* — toward the handle standing out of his own spine.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): And here's the thing that turns the last cut from a death into a birth.

---

## PAGE P — HE RECOGNIZES IT
*The reveal. 3 panels, tightening to the eyes.*

**PANEL 1.** EXTREME TIGHT: his fingers close on the handle — and the handle is *worn.* Grooved. Shaped to a grip.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The handle is familiar. Worn to the shape of a grip I've felt my whole life without once looking at it.

**PANEL 2.** His eyes. The understanding landing, going down with him.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **This knife has been in my back since 1996. It never came out.**

**PANEL 3.** Ghosted overlay on his kneeling body: the five prior wounds of the book — arm, spine, shoulder, scalp, neck — faintly traced, every one of them a **(RED)** thread running back to the single buried handle. All roads to this one knife.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Every cut in this whole book was the body remembering *this.* The one that stayed. The true spine of the book.

## PAGE Q — THE KEY TURNS / THE FLOOR DROPS ALL THE WAY
*FULL-BLEED SPLASH + a cascade strip. The plunge. Hands off to THE FLOOR.*

**MAIN IMAGE (full-bleed, vertical plunge).** The handle **turns in his back like a key in a lock** — and the grey floor of NOW gives out under his knee *completely.* No border. No half-second flash. He falls the whole distance down the page: past a thin white seam (the boy's veiled dark), past every floor, toward a hard blown-**(WHITE)** rectangle far below — a bright day, six lanes, a window already down. The **(RED)** trailing up off him as he drops.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): It's a key. It's been in the lock the whole time. And now it *turns.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): No snapping back. No half-second. It doesn't let me leave until it's finished.

**CASCADE STRIP (six small panels running down the side of the plunge).** The blades in order, falling past him as he reads every one on the way down: **1** the arm · **2** the spine · **3** the shoulder · **4** the scalp · **5** the neck (Tapioca over the car) · **6** the knife that stays. Each a flash of **(RED)** on grey.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I read every blade in order this time. All the way down.

> **HANDOFF →** the origin now tells straight through and slow: **THE FLOOR / '96 (pp. 44–56).** The errand, the glance, the bird, the wave that called the second car, Tapioca, the throw into traffic, the scalp crown to nape, the bared neck, the friend over the roof, the knife that stayed, the impossible drive home, the lot, the crowd, *"you're losing—."* Then he rises.

## PAGE R — RISEN / THE THESIS PAID IN FULL
*The payload. 4 panels, each carrying an image strong enough to hold the caption weight.*

**PANEL 1.** He breaks the surface out of the blown-**(WHITE)** of '96 — not snapped back, **RISEN,** the way you come up out of deep water once you've decided to. Still on one knee in the grey. Still bleeding. Knife still in his back.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I come up for the last time in the book. Not snapped back. Risen.

**PANEL 2.** MATCH / overlay: the '96 boy driving himself home on a ruined arm (blown white, muscle and bone at the wheel) ghosted over the NOW man on his knee holding the shield (grey). **Same set of the jaw. Thirty years apart. One posture.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A boy took a blade that should have ended him and did not end. Drove himself home leaking. Walked into a room full of light with the wound still open. And *voted to keep living.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He wasn't surviving by accident. He was being **forged.**

**PANEL 3 — the image that names the book.** The **SHIELD,** seen full for the first time — and its face is made of *his own scars.* The five slashes and the one buried knife, arranged into the shield's heraldry: **(RED)** lines on a black field, a coat of arms cut into a man.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The knife that stays didn't cripple me. It **credentialed** me.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I could be the shield in cut five *because* of the knife in cut six. The boy made the man. The wound made the wall.

**PANEL 4.** Quiet. His hand rests on the handle at his back — *not* pulling it out. Accepting it.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Some knives you don't get to take out. Some passengers you don't get to unbuckle.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): You just find out, thirty years later, at the worst hour, that the thing you thought was killing you the whole time is the exact thing that let you save your child.

## PAGE S — THE WITHDRAWAL / FADE TO WHITE
*Final page of the wound map. 4 panels. The hand leaves for the first time in six cuts.*

**PANEL 1.** The man on his knee, shield up, Keystone's silhouette safe behind it. At the edge of the frame — the hand. For the first time in the whole book, it **WITHDRAWS.** Draw it retreating into the black, its warm light guttering out.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The hand withdraws. Not because it lost — a thing that patient never fully loses.

**PANEL 2.** TIGHT on the retreating hand, the dimming warm light going to nothing.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Because it finally met the one thing it never accounts for and never can.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(small, receding, almost gone)* ...a wound that made a man stronger than the taking of him.

**PANEL 3.** LOW, wide, heroic: the man kneeling, knife in his back, shield up, the boy behind it — and the first real **(WHITE)** swelling in from the edges of the grey.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Tried to take my son. I lifted my shield. And the shield was made out of everything that ever tried to take me.**

**PANEL 4.** Compose the last image so that, read *down the spine of the book from the binding out,* the six cuts of **(RED)** spell not a man destroyed but a man **BUILT.** The **(WHITE)** takes the page.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Not a man being destroyed. A man being built.
> **HOLD ON THE KNIFE THAT STAYS, THE SHIELD THAT STAYS, THE BOY BEHIND IT. FADE TO (WHITE). HOLD THE WHITE.**
