# DARK SEVEN — TEASER SWAP TEST
*volume-three/teaser-swaps.md · proving the recipe is modular · obeys DESIGN-LAW.md*

**The claim:** the teaser is built with the same bones — *ignite → hold → engulf → void → reignite* — and you can swap the center to get a new teaser in the same visual language.

**The test:** build four teasers with four different centers, changing nothing but the center. Below is the result, plus an honest verdict on where it held and where it needed a patch.

> **Every prompt below assumes the DESIGN-LAW color-lock block is appended.** Attach the cross image whenever a cross appears, and always say **"do NOT redraw the cross."**

---

## THE BONES *(identical in all four — only §CENTER changes)*

```
Animate a seamless 10-second loop on a PURE BLACK background.
Colors: ONLY black, bright white, and bold pure blood red. No gold, no green,
no brown, no other colors.

CENTER: << the object goes here >>

Five distinct beats, clearly separated:
0.0-2.5s — the center is DARK, then IGNITES like a broken neon sign: flickers,
  stutters, fails to catch two or three times with electric sparks and a
  lightning arc, then surges to a full steady glow.
2.5-4.0s — it holds, glowing steadily.
4.0-6.5s — thick glossy BLOOD RED liquid pours from the top and COMPLETELY
  COVERS the center until it is entirely hidden under red; the glow dies out.
6.5-8.5s — the blood runs down and drains off the bottom in dripping strands
  while PURE BLACK fills from the top, until the frame is 100% SOLID BLACK and
  EMPTY — nothing visible at all.
8.5-10s — the center flickers back to life like neon reigniting, matching the
  very first frame so the loop repeats seamlessly.

The center NEVER changes shape — only light and blood move over it. High-contrast
film noir, grain, dark vignette. Low electrical hum and neon buzz under the
flicker. Portrait. Seamless loop.
```

## SWAP 1 — THE SHIELD OF SCARS
*Use for: Cut 5, the turn. The thesis in one object.*

```
CENTER: A heavy medieval shield, rendered in glowing white neon line-work,
whose face is a coat of arms made of SCARS — five long slash marks and one
knife buried to the handle, arranged as heraldry across it. The scars and the
knife glow BLOOD RED; the shield's outline glows clean WHITE.
```
**Note:** the shield's blood-red scars are load-bearing — they're the one thing the blood *doesn't* erase in beat 3. Add: *"as the blood covers it, the red scars are the last thing to disappear."*

---

## SWAP 2 — THE SEVEN
*Use for: the title card / the front of the book. The number that names it.*

```
CENTER: A large numeral 7, formed entirely from SEVEN tally-cut marks slashed
into the dark — six of them glowing clean WHITE, the seventh glowing BLOOD RED
and dripping. The 7 stands alone, carved rather than drawn, edges rough like a
blade dragged through white.
```
**Note:** a 7 is the best top-bleeding shape there is — blood pools on the flat top bar and pours down the blade. Add: *"blood collects along the top bar of the 7 and pours down its length."*

---

## SWAP 3 — THE KNIFE THAT STAYS
*Use for: Cut 6. The reveal. The most ominous of the set.*

```
CENTER: A single knife, buried to the handle, standing straight out of the
darkness — only the worn grip and a sliver of blade visible, glowing white neon,
with BLOOD RED welling thick and slow around where it enters the black. It does
not move. It never leaves.
```
**Note:** this one wants to *reverse* the beats — the blood should well UP from the knife rather than pour DOWN from the top. Patch beat 3: *"thick blood wells up and out from the base of the knife, spreading upward and outward until it engulfs the entire frame."* That's the one place the template needed a real change. See the verdict.

---

## SWAP 4 — THE RING OF SEVEN CROSSES
*Use for: the ambush / the Dark Seven descending.*

```
CENTER: Seven red neon Celtic crosses (attached reference — do NOT redraw the
cross) arranged in a circle, floating in the void, all facing inward, slowly
rotating. They ignite one at a time, in sequence, until all seven are burning.
```
**Note:** the sequential ignite is a natural fit for beat 1 — seven flickers instead of one. Add: *"they ignite one after another, seven times, before all holding steady."*

## THE VERDICT — does the template hold?

**Mostly yes. Three of four swapped clean.** The shield, the 7, and the ring of crosses all dropped straight into the bones with nothing but a one-line note. That's the modularity claim, confirmed.

**One real crack: the blood's direction is not universal.** The template assumes blood **falls from above** onto the center. That's right for anything that hangs or stands — a cross, a shield, a numeral. It's **wrong for the knife**, where the whole horror is that the blood comes *out of the wound* and rises. A pour-from-above knife teaser would read as generic; a well-up-from-the-blade teaser reads like the book.

**So the law gets one amendment.** Adding it now:

> **BEAT 3 — ENGULF, amended.** The blood covers the center. *How* it arrives depends on what the center is:
> - **Falls from above** onto objects that hang or stand (cross, shield, numeral, sign).
> - **Wells up from within** for objects that *are the wound* (the knife, a scar, a cut).
> The blood always wins the frame by the end of beat 3. Only its direction changes.

**Second, smaller finding:** the "engulf" beat is stronger when **one red element survives longest** — the scars on the shield, the seventh tally, the welling at the knife. The last thing the blood swallows should be *the reason the blood is there.* Worth making a habit; not yet a law.

*Test run. The bones hold. The template is real — with the blood-direction amendment folded into DESIGN-LAW.md.*
