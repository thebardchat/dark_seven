Dark Seven

A Shane Brazelton & Dr. Seen Presentation · Vol. III

Noir graphic novel cover / key art poster. Title: DARK SEVEN. Three colors ONLY — pure black, pure white, and one bold pure red (blood red, NOT maroon, NOT rust, NOT brown, NOT crimson). No other colors. No gold, no blue, no green, no warm tones. Visual: A man's bare back, spine running center of frame, a single knife-cut scar from shoulder to lower back. The scar glows faint white — the only grace in the image. Over the scar, a Celtic cross tattoo in bold black linework. The man faces away. His face is never shown. He is shadow and silhouette. Background: pure black. Atmosphere: heavy, oppressive, cinematic — like Frank Miller's SIN CITY meets a confessional. Panel borders visible, suggesting comic pages. Title treatment: DARK SEVEN in stark white block letters, film noir typography, top or bottom of frame. No decorative fonts — blunt, heavy, unavoidable. Red appears ONLY as a single thin line of blood tracing the scar. Nothing else is red. Mood: a man who took six cuts and is still standing. Not broken. Not hopeless. The cross is the answer. The scar is the question.
