# DARK SEVEN — CUTS 1–3
## Pencil-Ready Script · *The Arm · Down the Spine · The Shoulder Blade*
### the first three cuts — following THE FIRST WITNESS (pp. 1–5)
*Blocked in the master adaptation; here paneled to full density, at their real page numbers. Color law absolute: BLACK = the world/the dark · RED = blood, the only hue · WHITE = grace, earned and rare. NOW = rain-soaked, heaviest blacks, wet reflective streets. The veil is law. Voices: THE MAN, THE DARK (torn, red-dripping box), and the crew-selves ARC & WELD — fragments of the man, drawn as his own silhouette. `(RED)` = the one color on the panel. `(WHITE)` = an earned white-out.*

# CUT 1 — THE ARM
### NOW · the frame · first blood · teaches the reader to brace

## PAGE 6 — establishing *(rain, no color in it)*
*4 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** WIDE. The city black-and-white through a filthy, rain-streaked window — wet streets, heavy blacks, no color anywhere. The man at the glass, back to us, right arm held a little away from his body. *(Note the arm. It matters all book.)*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He runs a crew. That's what he tells himself.

**PANEL 2.** **ARC** at the threshold of the room — the man's own silhouette, warden's stance, a shadow-self with the same shoulders. A guard at a gate.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Arc keeps the gate. Nothing reaches him Arc hasn't cleared first.

**PANEL 3.** TIGHT on the door — a nameless grey file sliding under it into the room.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): So when the case slides under the door —

**PANEL 4.** Arc's head turning to it. Too slow.
> CAPTION (ARC): — Arc's supposed to stop it.

## PAGE 7 — the case + the hand
*3 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** The man crouched over the file. He knows its weight. He does not open it.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Some cases you don't investigate. Some you recognize.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *Nothing to do with him.* That's what the surface says. Everything to do with him.

**PANEL 2.** Bottom corner, small, where the streetlight gives out — **THE HAND.** Ringless. Patient. It has grown its own little warm light and hung it out front. It does not reach. *(no caption — let the hand sit.)*

**PANEL 3.** Arc at the gate, realizing.
> CAPTION (ARC): I can't hold this one. It's already inside. It came in wearing your own face.

## PAGE 8 — the ambush snaps shut
*5 panels, tightening.*

**PANEL 1.** A shape resolving out of the grey behind him.
**PANEL 2.** The shape has a hand.
**PANEL 3.** The hand has a line of light.
**PANEL 4.** His profile — not yet registering.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Thirty years running crews and I don't hear the room change —

**PANEL 5.** — through the sleeve, through the arm.
> SFX: **SHNK**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): — until the room's already closed.

## PAGE 9 — FIRST BLOOD *(the color law revealed)*
*FULL-BLEED SPLASH.*

**SINGLE IMAGE.** The first **(RED)** comes up out of him — one bright drop, then the string of them — red on a grey floor, red on a grey coat. The single hot wrong note in a black-and-white world. This is the reader's first color in the whole book. Make it hurt.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(torn box, dripping)* This is the shallow one. This is the one that barely hurt.

## PAGE 10 — the scar underneath *(the first drop-through)*
*3 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** EXTREME TIGHT. The blade doesn't find new skin — it touches an old thirty-year scar on the arm. Steel on the old line.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The blade doesn't find new skin. It finds the old line.

**PANEL 2.** The panel border becomes a **red slash;** through it, one blown-**(WHITE)** '96 fragment — a warm day, windows down, a woman driving, a bird returning over a door. A half-second glimpse.

**PANEL 3.** Back to grey — he slams the door on it. Fresh red bleeding over the old scar.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Every cut from here finds an old one. Every blade in this book has a door in it. And they only go *down.*

## PAGE 11 — brace *(the red thumbprint)*
*2 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** He picks up the nameless file with the arm that still works. A **(RED)** thumbprint left on the grey folder.
**PANEL 2.** Edge of frame: the hand hasn't moved.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *That was the shallow one.*
> **Hold on the red thumbprint. FADE.**

# CUT 2 — DOWN THE SPINE
### NOW → the first descent · the book's own binding · the veil goes up
**→ PRINT NOTE: pages 12–13 are a TWO-PAGE SPREAD built on the center gutter. The box-cutter line is inked down the actual seam of the book.**

## PAGES 12–13 — THE SPINE SPREAD
**LEFT PAGE (12).** The desk, NOW, heavier rain. Arm wrapped, a slow red dot still coming through. **WELD** at his shoulder — a maker's hands, flexing, wanting in. Same silhouette as the man.
> CAPTION (WELD): Point me at it. Whatever did this. I'll make it right. That's what I'm for.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Some of this is already done. You can't weld a door that closed thirty years ago.

**ACROSS THE GUTTER (13).** The hand fists the back of his coat, hauls it up, bares the spine. The blade draws — not a stab, a *drawing* — a slow bright **(RED)** box-cutter line **down the center gutter of the spread,** vertebra by vertebra. The wound and the book's spine are one object.
> SFX: *(no sound — just the warm coming up before the pain files its report)*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The crease the whole book is folded along.

## PAGES 14–15 — the veil goes up
*(veil render: white line on solid black. Symbol only. Nothing shown. Everything felt. Read as a descent.)*

**PANEL 1 (14).** Render flips to **white-line-on-black.** He expects '96. He doesn't get '96. He falls through his own oldest scar into a dark that isn't his.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Nothing here has edges. Nothing here has a name.

**PANEL 2 (14).** A bulb where a moon should hang, lying about being the moon.
**PANEL 3 (15).** Windows going black one at a time under a patient brush.
**PANEL 4 (15).** Grey chemical weather smothering a true sky. Small, scratched into a corner: *not allowed to close, wake up.*
> CAPTION (THE DARK): This is the why. You wanted to know why every cut in your body still bleeds. You've been bleeding down toward this the whole time.

## PAGE 16 — Weld, useless for the first time
*2 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** Down in the felt-dark, Weld's maker's hands reach for a seam to close, a bolt to set — and find no metal. Hands grasping smoke.
> CAPTION (WELD): There's no edge to weld.

**PANEL 2.** Weld's hands opening and closing on nothing. Something in him that never broke, making a sound going down.
> CAPTION (WELD): You cannot repair a wound that happened to someone else in a room you weren't allowed into.

## PAGE 17 — back up *(two reds run together)*
*2 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** He claws out with one working arm, breaks the surface into NOW, face down over the desk.
**PANEL 2.** The **(RED)** from the arm and the **(RED)** from the spine pool on the nameless folder until you can't tell the two wounds apart. His and the boy's. The same red.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): That was the second one. You've got four scars left. You know exactly how deep they go.
> **Hold on the two reds running together. FADE.**

# CUT 3 — THE SHOULDER BLADE
### NOW → the detective → deeper into the boy · the warm scratch that isn't a scratch
*The center of the book's grief. The veil is absolute here: the boy is never shown, never face-front; the phantom is only warmth and a hand, never a face. Draw around it, never at it.*

## PAGE 18 — two wounds old, one coming
*2 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** The desk. The man — arm and spine bleeding, bracing. **ARC** paces the gate: a guard who failed once and won't forgive himself.
> CAPTION (ARC): I should've stopped it. It's my whole job to stop it.

**PANEL 2.** Arc at the threshold, head down.
> CAPTION (ARC): How does a thing get past me.

## PAGE 19 — the third cut *(the horror of gentleness)*
*3 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** A hand pulls the coat aside and draws a line across the flat of his back.
**PANEL 2.** It lands like *nothing.* Like a warm scratch. Like being touched, almost kindly.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The ones that don't hurt are the ones already halfway to the bone.

**PANEL 3.** **(RED)** spreads warm across the flat of his back. Some animal part of him wants to lean back into the thing opening him.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *Warm* is just the body's word for a cut so clean the nerves haven't caught up.

## PAGE 20 — the detective's flash
*2 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** Red-slash border → blown-**(WHITE)** '96: the shirt yanked up, the warm line across the flat of his back mid-swing — the one cut in the whole massacre that didn't announce itself.
**PANEL 2.** Back to grey.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The cut that got the deepest reach in the whole war was the one that felt the least like a war. I learned that at fifteen. I forgot it.

## PAGES 21–22 — deeper into the boy
*(veil render. The center of the book's grief. Symbol only — no face, no fact, no act. The phantom is warmth and a hand; the boy is a small back-turned silhouette, never more.)*

**PANEL 1 (21).** He doesn't come back. He goes down to a floor of the boy's dark he didn't reach before. White-line-on-black.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The boy couldn't feel the blade. Because the blade came in warm.

**PANEL 2 (21).** Symbol: the thing that opened his son didn't arrive as a knife. It arrived as a **warm light, offered from off-panel** — being seen, being chosen, *forever,* said early and often. Never a face.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A cut that feels like love is the only cut a true heart won't defend itself against.

**PANEL 3 (22).** A good, honest structure — all its doors unlocked, because it never learned it needed locks — a small silhouette, back turned, leaning toward the warm off-panel glow it thought meant well.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): You cannot warn a body out of the wound that feels like being held.

**PANEL 4 (22).** Brave, urgent voices coming from the edges of the dark — small — hitting a boy who can only feel the warmth.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): They said: *this one is not what it says. This one is death.* And to a boy who could only feel the warm, they sounded like someone trying to take away the one hand that felt kind.

## PAGE 23 — Arc understands the flaw
*2 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** Arc at the gate, the understanding landing.
> CAPTION (ARC): It didn't come at the gate like a threat. It came like something you'd want to let in.

**PANEL 2.** Arc's hand on the gate he opened.
> CAPTION (ARC): I open for warmth. I was built to open for warmth. That's the flaw it found. In you. In him. Same gate.

## PAGE 24 — back up *(three reds)*
*3 panels.*

**PANEL 1.** Face over the desk. Arm, spine, shoulder — three **(RED)**s pooling, warm going cold.
**PANEL 2.** The three reds on the nameless folder, his and the boy's indistinguishable.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Three reds now. Mine and his. You still can't tell them apart.

**PANEL 3.** The hand at the edge of the frame — hasn't moved. And now he sees why it never had to reach.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): It doesn't grab. It offers.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): Three. Halfway. The warm ones are behind you now. The rest just hurt.
> **Hold on the three reds. FADE. → into CUT 4: THE SCALP.**
