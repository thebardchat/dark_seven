# VOLUME THREE — Build Doc / Skeleton
*Dark noir. Comic book. Black and white. The only color is blood.*

---

## THE RULE (unbreakable design law)
The world is **black and white**. The **only color that ever appears is red — bloodshed.** Not a sunset. Not a neon sign. Not a rose. Only what leaks out of a body. Break this rule nowhere.

## THE SPINE
The binding of the book **is** the scar down his back. The literal spine = the box-cutter line from '96. Every page hangs off that line.

## THE ENGINE
Each cut on the present-day detective triggers a break — a POV switch, a descent. **Each cut is deeper than the last.** He wears the same wounds he took in '96; when a blade finds an old scar, the page drops through time.

Three layers, braided:
1. **NOW** — the fight/case that's killing him *(the frame)*
2. **THE BOY** — the thread, obscured *(the why)*
3. **'96** — prom night, the origin *(the deepest floor)*

---

## THE WOUND MAP — this **is** the table of contents
*Present-day cut → what it opens. Order and depth locked to the real '96 sequence.*

### CUT 1 — THE ARM (through the window)
First blood. The ambush snaps shut. Teaches the reader to brace.
→ Opens: the case surfaces / the boy enters. The devil's hand at the edge of frame.

### CUT 2 — DOWN THE SPINE
The box-cutter line. The book's own binding.
→ Opens: first real descent into the boy's dark. **The veil goes up** — nothing named, everything felt.

### CUT 3 — THE SHOULDER BLADE
The warm scratch that isn't a scratch.
→ Opens: back to the detective, then *deeper* into the boy.

### CUT 4 — THE SCALP (back of the head, mid-fight)
Hair grabbed, cut crown to nape.
→ Opens: deepest dive into the boy yet. The huge fight rages in NOW while we fall.

### CUT 5 — THE NECK (the shield / the save)
The kill shot that doesn't land. Superman over the car. The hand that stops the blade.
→ **The turn: victim → protector.** *"Tried to take my son. I lifted my shield."*

### CUT 6 — THE BACK (the knife that stays)
Stabbed. Bleeding out. The floor drops all the way.
→ Opens: the origin told straight through — the boy that made the man.

## '96 — THE ORIGIN (source beats, held for writing)
- The errand. The father. *"Take this to the room. Don't touch it till you're there."* Main stretch, six lanes, ~4:15, windows down, warm day.
- The glance. The woman driving. The man in the passenger seat. The bird, returned. The rage.
- The wave that wasn't for them — the second carload called up.
- The long car whips in front, brakes. First cut through the window — arm to the bone.
- Tapioca: *"Get out — we're surrounded."* Out into it.
- One jumps, one swings (phone in hand, cracks his head). Picks the man up, slams him, chokes him.
- Looks up — they've got Tapioca. Lets go to help. Hit from the side. Picks that one up, throws him into oncoming traffic — horns.
- Shirt pulled up. Warm scratch — shoulder blade. Hair grabbed — scalp opened crown to neck. Head yanked back, neck bared for the finish.
- Tapioca, like Superman, over a car, into the man's head. The save.
- All red. Suffocating. Swinging every direction until a car brakes and screams and they scatter.
- The cost: Tapioca's ear cut off, stabbed in the back. Him — stabbed in the back.
- The drive. The gear. The arm — muscle and bone. Next lot. Out. Down. The crowd. *"You're losing—"* Fade.

## THE CREW = THE MIND (Mega Bots)
The detective's informants and muscle are the Mega Bots — because they're pieces of his own head. **Arc** at the gate (won't let everything through). **Weld**, the hand that does the work. He thinks he runs a crew. He's alone in a room with himself.
*Play as tragedy or as armor — your call.*

## THE VEIL
Obscured on purpose. Real specifics never touch the page. The rule of the veil: the reader feels it in the chest and is never handed a fact. **Nothing to do with it — everything to do with it.** The surface case hides the real one. That's noir working exactly as built.
