# DARK SEVEN — THE FLOOR
## Pencil-Ready Script · *'96, The Origin — Told Straight*
### the deepest floor of the wound map · first blade to last · unbroken
*This is where Cut 6's plunge lands. Told straight through — no cuts back, no drop-throughs, no veil. The one sequence in the book with no timeline-jumping: the origin, whole. Cut 6 Page R **rises** from the last frame of this chapter. Replaces/expands master pp. 44–56.*

> **RENDER — '96.** High-key, blown-out **daylight-white,** thin black line, hard shadow — a bright day overexposed to near-white. **Blood is the only color, always,** and reads its brightest against all that white. No greys of NOW here; this is the other world, the bottom, lit like a memory that won't dim. `(RED)` = the one color on the panel.
> **MOTIF — THE HORNS.** Once the first blade lands, a chorus of car horns threads the whole sequence and **never stops for any of it.** Letter them small and constant in the gutters — `HOOONK` — so the reader can't get away from them either.
> **THE KNIFE THAT STAYS.** Every blade here is a slash that draws and pulls away — *except the knife in the back.* That one goes to the handle and stays; he drives home with it in him. It is the knife Cut 6 reveals. Render it distinctly.

## PAGE T — SIX LANES, 4:15
*3 panels. The afternoon that has no idea what it's about to become.*

**PANEL 1.** WIDE ESTABLISH — blown white. A six-lane road, one turning lane down the middle, shimmering in bright afternoon. A single truck moving through all that white. Windows down. **No color anywhere. Not yet.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Warm day. Windows down. The kind of afternoon with no idea what it's about to become.

**PANEL 2.** INT. TRUCK CAB — a father's hand passing the boy a small, ordinary something. Keep the father spare, archetype not portrait. His voice flat and final.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *Take this to the room. Don't touch it till you're there.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): An errand. That's all it was supposed to be.

**PANEL 3.** The boy driving, one arm resting in the open window, music you can almost hear, **TAPIOCA** riding shotgun. Two kids in the white. Ordinary as bread.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A boy running a thing across town on a bright day.

## PAGE U — THE GLANCE
*4 panels. Less than a look, taken wrong.*

**PANEL 1.** Two cars breathing side by side at speed. The other car pacing them — a woman driving, a man folded low in the passenger seat.

**PANEL 2.** TIGHT: the boy's eyes flick over. A glance. Less than a glance.

**PANEL 3.** The man in the passenger seat takes it wrong — the kind of man just *waiting* to take it wrong. The bird comes up over his door.

**PANEL 4.** The boy — young, dumb, alive — flips it right back. And behind the man's eyes the rage catches, hot and instant and out of all proportion to a look on a Tuesday.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Out of all proportion to a glance. Some men are just holding the match.

---

## PAGE V — THE WAVE
*4 panels. The tell nobody clocks in time.*

**PANEL 1.** The passenger hauls himself up onto the window sill, half out of the moving car now, bucking, yelling, hurling something at the boy's truck.
> SFX: **BAP** *(something off the windshield)*

**PANEL 2.** He waves — *come on, up here* — and the boy reads it as meant for him.

**PANEL 3.** But it isn't. Follow the wave *back:* a second car, long and full, coming up fast from behind like dogs let off a chain.

**PANEL 4.** REVEAL — a long stretched car, **six, seven men** inside it, faces set. This was never one man's temper.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A hunt. With more teeth than the road could see yet.

---

## PAGE W — BOXED / CUT 1 — THE ARM
*4 panels. The first blade. The first color.*

**PANEL 1.** The long car whips around in front and stands on the brakes. The truck boxed in white and steel.
> SFX: **SKREEEE**

**PANEL 2.** EXTREME TIGHT — a shape at the boy's open window, a line of light in its hand. Before his brain can file the shape as a *blade —*

**PANEL 3 — BIG.** It's through the open window and through the arm. Muscle, then the deep cold wrongness of bone. **FIRST BLOOD** — the first **(RED)** in the whole white world, sheeting off the elbow onto the door, onto the lane.
> SFX: **SHNK**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): First blood on the highway.

**PANEL 4.** And the horns start, and this is the last panel before they never stop.
> SFX: **HOOOONK**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The horns will not stop for any of it.

---

## PAGE X — "GET OUT"
*2 panels. Two words that end the afternoon.*

**PANEL 1.** Tapioca, wild-eyed, grabbing him — the shout that ends everything.
> TAPIOCA: **GET OUT — WE'RE SURROUNDED.**

**PANEL 2.** WIDE. Doors flung open. Two boys stepping *out* into the middle of six screaming lanes, the whole carload closing on them from every side. `HOOONK` in the gutters.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): No version where you stay in the box. So you go out — into it.

---

## PAGE Y — THE FIRST TWO
*4 panels. The only math he's ever known.*

**PANEL 1.** One jumps him. A second swings — a **phone still in the fist,** plastic cracking off the side of his head. A white flash, the taste of pennies.
> SFX: **KRAK**

**PANEL 2.** He gets his hands into the first man anyway. Picks him clean up off the road.

**PANEL 3.** SLAMS him down onto the asphalt. Drops a knee. A hand closes on the throat and *squeezes* — the man's heels drumming the road.
> SFX: **kh—kh—kh**

**PANEL 4.** And the boy looks up.

---

## PAGE Z — THEY'VE GOT TAPIOCA / THE THROW
*5 panels. He spends the sure thing to save his friend.*

**PANEL 1.** What he sees: they've got **Tapioca** — surrounded, going under.

**PANEL 2.** He lets go of the sure thing. The choke released, the beaten man forgotten.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I let go of the sure thing to save my friend. The only math I've ever known how to do.

**PANEL 3.** The instant he turns loose — a hit from the blind side rocks him.
> SFX: **WHUMP**

**PANEL 4.** He gets *under* the new one. Lifts him clean off his feet.

**PANEL 5 — WIDE.** And **throws him into the oncoming lane.** A body in the air over white asphalt.
> SFX: **SKREEE — HOOOONK** *(brakes, then horns swallowing whatever sound the road makes)*

---

## PAGE AA — CUTS 2 & 3 — THE SPINE, THE SHOULDER BLADE
*3 panels. The warm scratch that isn't a scratch. Where the book's spine is born.*

**PANEL 1.** From behind — a fist yanks his shirt up, bares his back.

**PANEL 2.** The line drawn **down the spine.** Not a stab — a *drawing,* slow and bright. So soft-feeling it barely registers.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): So soft-feeling and so wrong.

**PANEL 3.** Another line **across the shoulder blade.** The **(RED)** running warm into his waistband before the pain even files its report.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The box-cutter line. The one the whole book hangs its binding on.

---

## PAGE BB — CUT 4 — THE SCALP
*3 panels. Where the night was always driving.*

**PANEL 1.** A fist finds his hair and **hauls.** The head snapping back.

**PANEL 2.** The blade opens the back of his head, **crown to nape** — one long, cold unzipping.
> SFX: **rrrRIP**

**PANEL 3 — BIG.** The world tips. Sky where the asphalt should be. A warm **(RED)** curtain coming down over one eye. His head wrenched back, throat pulled long, bared to the white sky. The finish loaded.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): This is where the night was always driving. Neck open. Waiting for the last cut.

---

## PAGE CC — CUT 5 — THE SAVE
*FULL-BLEED SPLASH. The origin of the image the whole book is built on. Blown white, blood the only color.*

**SINGLE IMAGE.** **TAPIOCA comes over the roof of a car.** Over it — one ear already half gone and pouring **(RED)**, the back of him already opened and bleeding, spending a body he has no business still standing in — clean over the whole car, arms wide, *like something torn out of a comic book dropped onto a real highway,* landing on the man with the blade, into his head. **The last cut never falls.** The throat closes.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Somebody who had every reason to save his own skin spent it instead. Came over the car for me.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I was young. I learned the only theology I've ever fully trusted: **the finish doesn't have to land.**

---

## PAGE DD — ALL RED / THE KNIFE THAT STAYS
*4 panels. Not choreography anymore — a drowning. And the one blade that never leaves.*

**PANEL 1.** He comes back down into his own body inside a lake of **(RED)**. No choreography now — a drowning. Swinging every direction at once, blind in one eye, can't tell his blood from theirs from the road's. Two shapes in a red fog throwing fists that land wet.

**PANEL 2 — TIGHT.** In the fog, a blade goes into his back — and where every other cut drew its line and pulled away, **this one goes to the handle and STAYS.** Buried. No motion out.
> SFX: **thunk** *(small, final — the sound the whole book will remember)*

**PANEL 3.** One more car locks up its brakes and **SCREAMS** — long, human, wrong.
> SFX: **SKREEEEEEE**

**PANEL 4.** Something in that sound breaks the spell. The seven scatter back into the traffic they came out of, gone as fast as they came.

---

## PAGE EE — THE COST
*3 panels. Two boys upright in a stopped highway, counting their own leaks.*

**PANEL 1.** **Tapioca:** an ear cut clean off. A knife left in his back. Cut around the waist, the leg. **Standing.**

**PANEL 2.** **Him:** the arm open to the bone. The spine. The shoulder blade. The scalp, crown to nape. The knife still in his back. **Standing.**

**PANEL 3 — WIDE.** The two of them upright in the middle of a stopped highway, white and bleeding, the horns still going. Three of the seven down in the road they thought was theirs.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Two of us. Standing. Counting our own leaks.

---

## PAGE FF — THE DRIVE
*4 panels. The part nobody would believe if it weren't true.*

**PANEL 1.** Into the vehicle. A hand on a **manual gearbox** — the knife still in his back.

**PANEL 2.** EXTREME TIGHT — hand to the knob, and every shift a fresh scream from an arm that's more open than closed. Muscle and bone arguing the whole way through every gear.
> SFX: *(no sound — just the arm)*

**PANEL 3.** He drives. **(RED)** filling the seat. **(RED)** on the wheel. The white road unspooling ahead.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Muscle and bone arguing through every gear. And I drove.

**PANEL 4.** The next lot. The truck stopping crooked. The door opening — and the ground coming up to meet him.

---

## PAGE GG — THE CROWD / FADE
*3 panels. The bottom of the whole book. Cut 6 Page R rises from here.*

**PANEL 1.** He's down. A crowd gathers the way crowds do — too late and too loud. Tapioca somewhere near, holding the side of his own head.

**PANEL 2.** A face over him, a mouth moving, sound going far away. The last thing he hears.
> VOICE (over him): You're losing—

**PANEL 3 — near-full.** The white blows out, and the last thing in the world is the one color that was ever in it.
> **FADE TO (RED). THEN TO BLACK.**
