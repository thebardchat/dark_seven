# DARK SEVEN — CUT 4 → CUT 5
## Pencil-Ready Script · *The Descent · The Flip · The Shield*
*The climax of the wound map. Every panel drawn from Shane's manuscript. Color law absolute: BLACK = the world/the dark · RED = blood, the only hue · WHITE = grace, earned and rare. Veil is law: the phantom is a hand and a light, never a face; Keystone is never face-front; the boy's dark is symbol only. `(RED)` = the one color on the panel. `(WHITE)` = an earned white-out.*

> **PRINT PRODUCTION — THE FLIP.** Pages **C through G** (Cut 4, the descent) **print rotated 180°.** The reader physically turns the book upside down to read the bottom of the fall. Folios/page numbers invert with them. On page **J** (Cut 5, the turn) the book **rights itself** — the reader rotates it back. Binding and imposition must account for this; flag to the printer early. This is the whole book's signature device: the turn of the book *is* the turn of the man.

# CUT 4 — THE SCALP
### NOW · the huge fight · the deepest dive · crown to nape · the world goes upside down

## PAGE C-1 *(right-side up — the last upright page before the flip)*
*6 panels. The file has become a war. No office, no rain-on-glass — only bodies in the grey.*

**PANEL 1.** WIDE, low. A grey no-place — no walls we can place, just dark and figures. The man, center, mid-swing with the arm that still works, the right arm held wrong even now. Faceless attackers coming out of the black on every side. Two dressings already showing **(RED)** — the wrapped forearm, a dot bleeding through at the spine.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The file did what files like this do. It stopped being paper and became a fight.

**PANEL 2.** TIGHT on his working fist connecting with a jaw. Ink-spray, no red — just a tooth and sweat.
> SFX: **KRAK**

**PANEL 3.** Behind him: **ARC** — his own silhouette, warden's stance — at a gate that's already off its hinges, screaming into the dark. Draw Arc as *him,* a shadow-self, same shoulders.
> CAPTION (ARC): Too many. TOO MANY —

**PANEL 4.** **WELD** — another shadow-self, defined by the maker's hands — swinging into the crowd, connecting with half of it. Same face as the man, half-lit.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The whole room of myself out of the walls at once.

**PANEL 5.** OTS from behind the man — the crowd doesn't thin. It doubles. More shapes in the grey than the last panel.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): And still not enough. They were only ever pieces of one tired man. The dark brought a crowd.

**PANEL 6.** Small, bottom corner — the recurring beat: **the hand at the edge of the frame.** Ringless. Open. Unmoving. Its little off-panel warm light just touching the panel edge. It is not fighting. It never fights.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(torn box, dripping)* Let them tire you out. I have all night.

## PAGE C-2 *(right-side up — the grip, the razor)*
*4 panels, tightening to the cut.*

**PANEL 1.** WIDE. A hand — one of theirs, faceless — closes in the hair at his **crown** and *hauls.* His neck snapping back. No blade yet.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): They don't grab your head unless they mean to open it.

**PANEL 2.** EXTREME TIGHT on the crown of his head. The razor's line of light touches skin.
> SFX: *(silence — a beat with no sound)*

**PANEL 3.** The razor travels — crown toward nape — one long cold seam. **(RED)** wells up along the line, the first bright wrong note.
> SFX: **rrrrRIP**

**PANEL 4.** His eyes, wide, as the horizon begins to tilt in the panel behind them. The grey world starting to lean.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Everything I've ever known about which way is down —
> CAPTION (THE MAN): — files for divorce all at once.

## PAGE C-3 — THE FLIP *(FULL-BLEED SPLASH — this page prints inverted; reader rotates the book NOW)*

**SINGLE IMAGE.** The world has turned all the way over. The grey street hangs *above* him like a ceiling of water. **(RED)** sheets *upward* — off the scalp, up over his brow, up into one eye, defying the page. His head wrenched back on the fist, throat pulled long, **neck bared to a sky that has become a floor.** This is the posture the whole night was driving toward: head back, neck open, the finish loaded. Compose so that when the reader rotates the book, the blood still reads as running *up.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *(placed to read correctly inverted)* Sky where the asphalt should be. This is where the night was always going.
> **[PRINT NOTE: page inverted 180°. Folio inverted. The reader is now holding the book upside down.]**

---

## PAGE D — THE DEEPEST DIVE *(inverted · veil render · white line on black)*
*This is the emotional floor of the whole book. Symbol only. Nothing shown. Everything felt. 3 tall panels, read top-to-bottom in the reader's rotated hands.*

**PANEL 1.** Render inverts to **white line on solid black.** A vast cathedral-structure — good, honest, load-bearing — hanging **upside down from its own foundation.** Every window in it **blacked out,** not one left lit to argue through. No figures yet. Just the inverted architecture in the void.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Upside down isn't a camera trick. It's the true shape of the thing. It's where grief actually lives.

**PANEL 2.** Deep in the inverted dark: a **small silhouette** — Keystone — at the lip of the structure's highest tower. **Back to us. Never a face.** Above him (which is now below), the drop opens like a mouth. Off-panel, from the black, a single **warm light source** — the only "presence" of the phantom. No hand even, here. Just the warm glow it hangs out front.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(warm, unhurried)* The drop is a door. The fall is a way out. Stepping off is the same as being held.

**PANEL 3.** The man — tiny, inverted, an ocean and thirty years away — reaches across the void toward the small figure. His fingers **do not close the distance.** Draw the gap. The gap is the whole panel.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I can feel the exact inch he's standing on.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): And I cannot touch it.

---

## PAGE E — THE ROOM COMES APART *(inverted · the crew scatters)*
*4 panels. His mind falls to pieces with the world.*

**PANEL 1.** **ARC**'s gate tears loose and falls up-into-the-black. Arc's shadow-self coming apart at the edges.
> CAPTION (ARC): The gate's gone. The gate's — *gone* —

**PANEL 2.** **WELD**'s maker's hands open — on nothing. Draw them grasping smoke, three times in one panel if you can stagger it, each grip closing on void.
> CAPTION (WELD): Nothing to hold. Nothing to hold. *Nothing to hold.*

**PANEL 3.** WIDE, inverted. Every fragment he ever called a crew revealed as **furniture in a room with one man in it** — chairs, a desk, the informants, all of it tumbling past him toward a sky that used to be a floor. The man alone at the center of the falling room.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He's alone. He was always alone.

**PANEL 4.** TIGHT, inverted: his face, upside down, straining upward-that-is-downward, listening.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A man in an inverted room, reaching up-that-is-down for a boy he can *hear breathing* and cannot find.

---

## PAGE F — THE BOTTOM *(inverted · the lowest the book goes)*
*The floor of the descent. 2 panels + a held beat. Both timelines share one bared throat.*

**PANEL 1.** SPLIT the inverted page down the gutter without a border — LEFT: the man in the grey, head back, throat long, the razor at his crown beginning its travel toward the throat. RIGHT: the inverted dark, Keystone's small silhouette, one inch of tower left under his foot, the drop yawning. **Two finishes, thirty years apart, on one held breath.** The only color on the page: the **(RED)** running up from the scalp.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Both necks. Two finishes on one held breath.

**PANEL 2.** At the very bottom edge of the inverted page (which reads as the *top* to the rotated reader) — a shape breaking over the roof of a car. We can't tell yet what it is. Just motion, coming.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Something is always, in this family, coming over the top of the car.

**PANEL 3 — small, black, held.** Near-empty panel. One drop of **(RED)** suspended in the void, refusing to fall in the right direction.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(quiet)* This is the bottom. There's nothing under you but the last cut.
> **[PRINT NOTE: this is the last inverted page. The book rights on the next turn.]**

# CUT 5 — THE NECK / THE SHIELD
### the kill shot that doesn't land · over the car · the turn: victim → protector · WHITE enters

## PAGE H *(still inverted at the top of the page — the razor traveling)*
*3 panels. We ride the held breath one beat longer, then it breaks.*

**PANEL 1.** *(inverted)* TIGHT: the razor's line of light, a hair from the bared throat. **(RED)** already beading where it rests.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The razor moving. The drop opening like a mouth.

**PANEL 2.** *(inverted)* WIDE: the shape from page F, fully in motion now, clearing the roof of a car — airborne.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): And something comes over the top of the car.

**PANEL 3.** *(the page begins to right — draw this panel at a 90° cant, the world mid-rotation)* Motion blur, the horizon swinging back toward true.
> SFX: **—hnnGH—**

---

## PAGE I — '96, THE HAND THAT STOPS THE BLADE *(FULL-BLEED SPLASH · blown-white daylight · book now RIGHT-SIDE UP)*

**SINGLE IMAGE.** Render slams to **'96: high-key, blown-out daylight-white,** thin black line, hard shadow. **TAPIOCA** — an ear already half gone and pouring **(RED)**, the back of him already opened and bleeding — comes **clean over the roof of a car,** airborne, arms out, *like something torn out of a comic book and dropped onto a real highway,* landing on the man with the razor. **The last cut does not fall.** The blood is the only color in a world of white. Give this the full page — it is the theology of the whole book in one image.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I was fifteen. I learned the only theology I've ever fully trusted.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **The finish doesn't have to land. Somebody can get a hand in. Somebody can come over the car.**

---

## PAGE J — THE TURN *(the world rights · the book is right-side up · 4 panels)*
*The hinge the whole book is folded along. He stops being the neck.*

**PANEL 1.** Back to NOW, grey. The man, still bent back — but his eyes change. Something decades old turning over behind them.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I forgot it, the way you forget the thing that saved you. I've been falling all five cuts *because* I forgot it.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I remember it now.

**PANEL 2.** His working hand comes UP — reaching into his own inverted sky. Fingers spread against the grey ceiling-of-water.
> SFX: **GHHNN—**

**PANEL 3.** WIDE. The world **groans and rights itself** — the grey ceiling lifting back into a sky, his feet finding a floor that agrees to be a floor. Draw the rotation as a physical wrench of the whole panel-world. (The reader has already turned the book back with page I.)
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A man who decides to be the shield instead of the wound just changed which way gravity works in his own book.

**PANEL 4.** LOW, heroic angle — first time in the chapter we look *up* at him. On his feet. Bleeding from four places. Standing.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I was the boy who got saved by someone coming over a car.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Now I'm the one coming over the car.

---

## PAGE K — THE SHIELD *(FULL-BLEED SPLASH · first real WHITE · veil holds)*

**SINGLE IMAGE.** Down in the veiled dark — where all chapter he could feel the inch and never touch it — his hand **finally lands.** Not on the phantom (you cannot punch a thing made of warmth) — on the **inch,** between Keystone's small back-turned silhouette and the door-that-isn't-a-door. He lifts not a fist but a **SHIELD.** And from behind/through it breaks the first real **(WHITE)** in the book — a locked, blazing, clean white light, the ugliest-beautiful rescue there is, hands that reached into the pit and pulled a boy up into hard clean light. Compose: black pit below, white breaking above, the **(RED)** of his four wounds the only color, the shield the fulcrum between dark and light. Keystone still never face-front — a silhouette rising into white.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Not on the phantom. On the inch. A father's whole body arriving thirty years and an ocean late — and somehow, by the only mercy that runs backward through time, exactly on time.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Tried to take my son. I lifted my shield.**

---

## PAGE L — THE CREW REASSEMBLES AS ONE *(SPLASH · every fragment turns the same way)*

**SINGLE IMAGE.** The scattered room comes back together around the one thing a mind will always reassemble around: **a child to stand in front of.** **ARC**'s blown gate becomes a **WALL.** **WELD**'s empty hands — that could find no wound to close — find the one job left to a hand that can't fix the past: *guard the future.* Draw every shadow-self of the man — Arc, Weld, the informants, all the pieces — **turned to face the same direction,** shoulder to shoulder, all of them him, all of them a shield, Keystone's silhouette small and safe behind the line. He thought he ran a crew. He was alone in a room with himself. Now: one man, every piece faced the same way.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Not less alone. Differently alone.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): One man. Every piece of me faced the same direction. All of it a shield.

---

## PAGE M — NOT OVER, BUT TURNED *(the coda into Cut 6 · 4 panels · fade to white)*
*The fight in NOW isn't won. One more scar coming — the deepest, the one that stays.*

**PANEL 1.** The shield held high, the boy's silhouette behind it, white light steady. But at the far edge of the frame — small, patient, unmoved — **the hand.** A thing that patient does not leave because a shield went up.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A knife still coming. One more scar. The deepest. The one that stays.

**PANEL 2.** TIGHT on the two kinds of **(RED)** in one panel: blood spraying off an enemy blade (left) / blood running down the man's own shield-arm, spilled *on purpose* to stay between the dark and the child (right).
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The blood means two things now. What the enemy spills — and what a father pours out on purpose to hold the door.

**PANEL 3.** WIDE, pulling back: the man, the shield, the boy, the white light — and the hand at the edge, waiting. One floods the frame. The other holds the door.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(no drip this time — steady, cold)* A shield doesn't send me away. It only decides how deep the last one has to go.

**PANEL 4.** The white light swells until it takes the panel.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Hold on the shield, up. The boy behind it.**
> **FADE — not to black. TO (WHITE). HOLD THE WHITE. → into CUT 6: THE KNIFE THAT STAYS.**
