## How to use

Attach your **cross image** so the neon crosses stay on-model, paste a scene, and go. Each scene already ends with a compact style tail. If a generation drifts on color, paste the **full house style block** below and the **color-lock note** from `teaser-prompt.md`.

These are built as **splash pages / key art** — one per big beat of the book. Push them loud; that's the point.

---

## THE HOUSE STYLE (the locked look — paste this if you want maximum control)

```
Black-and-white world — the ONLY color anywhere in the image is blood red;
absolutely everything else is pure high-contrast grayscale, including skin.
Glowing red neon Celtic crosses (flared arms, double ring, four-pointed star)
float throughout the scene. Blood drips from the top of the frame and pools
below. 1940s film-noir, heavy chiaroscuro, smoke and haze, sharp film grain,
dramatic cinematic splash-page composition. No blue, no green, no gold, no
color of any kind except blood red. Bold, mythic, over-the-top.
```

## THE SCENES

### 1 — TAPIOCA OVER THE CAR *(the '96 save — the money shot)*
```
A man in a torn 1940s suit launches fully airborne over the roof of a speeding
car on a six-lane highway, arms spread like a comic-book superhero, his coat
flaring behind him like a cape, one ear torn half off and throwing an arc of
blood through the air, mid-tackle onto a knifeman — the blade frozen one inch
from a young man's bared throat. Headlights blaze, safety glass explodes into a
cloud, tires scream. Caught at the very peak of the leap.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 2 — THROWN INTO ONCOMING TRAFFIC
```
A huge man in a fedora and trench coat hurls an attacker straight up into the
air over a highway; the body is silhouetted helpless and spread-eagled against
the blazing chrome grille and headlights of an oncoming car, horns screaming,
other figures brawling in the smoke around them, glass and grit suspended in
mid-air. The instant before impact, everything frozen.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 3 — THE UPSIDE-DOWN BRAWL *(the flip / Cut 4)*
```
An entire street brawl turned completely upside down — gravity inverted, the
wet grey asphalt hangs overhead like a ceiling of black water, the sky is the
floor. A man in a hat and coat is wrenched backward by his hair, throat pulled
long and bared to a straight razor, blood sheeting UPWARD off his scalp in
defiance of gravity. Attackers, shattered crates, and cigarette smoke all hang
inverted. Red neon Celtic crosses float in the vertiginous void. Surreal,
dizzying, dreamlike Escher noir.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 4 — THE SHIELD OF SCARS *(the turn)*
```
A battered detective in a shredded trench coat drops to one knee and raises an
enormous shield forged from his own wounds — the shield's face is a coat of arms
made entirely of glowing red slash-scars and a single buried knife. Enemy blades
shatter against it in showers of bright sparks. Behind him blazes a giant red
neon Celtic cross like a halo, and a small child's silhouette shelters safely
behind the shield. Mythic, operatic, holy-war grandeur.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 5 — ARC & WELD, THE CREW THAT IS ONE MAN
```
A lone man in a fedora fights a faceless horde in a smoke-filled warehouse — but
his own shadow-selves have stepped out of his body to fight beside him:
identical figures with the exact same face, one standing guard in a doorway like
a stone-faced warden, one with glowing red maker's hands, all of them clearly
him, all turned the same direction. The mob doubles endlessly into the haze.
Eerie, multiplied — one man who is an entire army of himself.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 6 — THE KNIFE THAT STAYS *(the reveal)*
```
A wounded detective kneels at the center of a warehouse floor, a knife buried to
the handle in his back, dark blood welling around it — yet he is rising, jaw set,
a shield still lifted overhead. A red neon Celtic cross reflects in the blade's
steel. At the far edge of the frame, a pale patient hand withdraws into the
black. Bodies lie strewn around him. Bleeding out and unbroken. Tragic, defiant,
operatic.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 7 — THE DARK SEVEN DESCEND *(the ambush)*
```
A long stretched black sedan skids sideways to a screeching stop, boxing in a
pickup truck on a six-lane highway at dusk, its doors flung open as SEVEN
silhouetted attackers pour out at once beneath a sky full of floating red neon
Celtic crosses. First blood sprays from an arm hanging out the truck window as a
blade rakes through it to the bone. Overwhelming, outnumbered — the ambush
snapping shut like a trap.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 8 — ONE MAN AGAINST EVERYONE *(the big set-piece)*
```
A single massive man in a fedora and trench coat stands at the dead center of an
all-out brawl in a smoke-choked warehouse, mid-swing, a punch landing so hard it
ripples a visible shockwave through the air and sends an attacker flying. Bodies
are hurled in every direction, electric arcs crackle off dozens of floating red
neon Celtic crosses, blood rains from the ceiling and pools across shattered
crates. Epic, chaotic — one man versus a hundred.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```

### 9 — THE CHOKE ON THE ASPHALT *(raw and close)*
```
A young man slams an attacker down onto highway asphalt and pins him by the
throat, one knee driven into his chest, the attacker's heels drumming the road,
headlights streaking past in long blurred trails, safety glass raining down
around them, a single red neon Celtic cross burning in the dark overhead. Raw,
intimate, savage, close-up.
— black-and-white world, blood red the ONLY color, floating red neon Celtic
crosses, blood dripping from the top of frame, 1940s film noir, heavy grain,
cinematic splash page.
```
