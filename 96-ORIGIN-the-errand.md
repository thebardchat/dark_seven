# '96 — THE ORIGIN
### The deepest floor of the wound map · told straight · black, white, and blood
*Source: VOLUME-THREE-skeleton.md → "'96 — THE ORIGIN (source beats)." Nothing invented; shaped from Shane's own beats. Blood is the only color that touches the page.*

---

**FADE IN — SIX LANES, 4:15, BLOWN-OUT WHITE**

Warm day. Windows down. Bright afternoon light overexposed to near-white, the whole world blown out and innocent. The kind of afternoon that has no idea what it's about to become. His father's voice still in the truck with him, flat and final the way fathers are: *Take this to the room. Don't touch it till you're there.* An errand. That's all it was supposed to be. A boy running a thing across town on a bright day.

**THE GLANCE**

Two cars breathing side by side at speed. A woman driving. A man folded in the passenger seat. Nothing — a look, less than a look. But the man takes it wrong the way some men are just *waiting* to take it wrong, and the bird comes back over the door, and behind the bird comes the rage, hot and instant and out of all proportion to a glance on a Tuesday.

**THE WAVE**

And here's the tell nobody clocks in time: the wave isn't *for* them. It goes back. A second carload, called up from behind like dogs off a chain. This was never one man's temper. This was a hunt with more teeth than the road could see yet.

**CUT 1 — THE ARM (through the window)**

The long car whips in front and stands on the brakes. Boxed. And before the boy's brain can file the shape in the man's hand as a *blade,* it's already through the open window and through the arm — muscle, then the deep cold wrongness of bone — and the red comes up all at once, sheeting off the elbow onto the door, onto the lane, into all that white. First blood on the highway. The horns start. **The horns will not stop for any of it.**

**"GET OUT — WE'RE SURROUNDED."**

Tapioca's voice. Two words that end the afternoon. And there's no version where you stay in the box, so they go *out* — out of the doors and into the middle of six screaming lanes with the whole carload closing.

**THEY'VE GOT TAPIOCA.**

He sees it before he feels anything of his own. They've got his friend surrounded, and they are *opening* him — the ear gone, a blade in the back low by the lumbar, a cut drawn front-to-back around the waist, another around the thigh, the tricep. And the boy does the only math he's ever known: he doesn't run away from it. He *runs at it.* Straight into the swarm, for Tapioca.

**THROUGH TWO CARS**

One peels off to tackle him. He gets under the man instead, picks him clean up off his feet — and *carries* him, runs him through two cars in the stopped traffic and slams him down onto the second lane, drops on him, and starts punching the life out of him, both fists, not stopping.

**CUT 2, 3 & 4 — THE SPINE, THE SHOULDER BLADE, THE SCALP**

And that is when they get him. Head down over the man he's beating, his own back turned — a blade drawn down the spine, another across the shoulder blade, the warm scratch that isn't a scratch, blood running into his waistband before the pain files its report. Then a fist in his hair, hauling his head back, baring his throat to the sky. The box-cutter line down the spine — the one the book will hang its whole binding on. The neck bared. The finish loaded. He is a second from dead, still swinging.

**THE SCALP — THE FINISH LOADED**

The razor opens the back of his head where the hair's yanked — crown to nape, one long unzipping — and the world tips, sky where the asphalt should be, a warm red curtain coming down over one eye. His neck long and open to the sky. This is the finish. This is where the night was always driving. One cut left, and it's coming.

**CUT 5 — THE SHIELD (the save)**

And Tapioca comes over the *front* of a car.

Torn to pieces already — an ear gone, opened front and back, a knife in his own spine — and he *flies,* over the hood of a car like something out of a comic book, and hits the man with the blade square in the **forehead** and drops him where he stands. The last cut never falls. The neck closes. The boy comes back down into his own body inside a lake of red. *The save.* The one who was more torn apart than anyone spent what he had left to keep his friend from dying.

**ALL RED**

After that it's not choreography anymore. It's a drowning. Swinging every direction at once, blind in one eye, can't tell his blood from theirs from the road's, both of them just two shapes in a red fog throwing fists that land wet — until one more car locks up its brakes and *screams,* long and human, and something in that sound finally breaks the spell, and the seven scatter back into the traffic they came out of.

**THE COST**

Tapioca: an ear cut clean off. A blade in the back low by the lumbar. Opened front-to-back around the waist. Around the thigh. The tricep. Torn open in five places — and he still *flew over a car* to save his friend. *Standing.*
Him: the arm to the bone. The spine. The shoulder blade. The scalp crown to nape. A knife in his own back. Standing.

Two of them, upright in the middle of a stopped highway, counting their own leaks. Three of the seven down in the road they thought was theirs.

**THE DRIVE**

Then the part nobody would believe if it weren't true. Into the vehicle. Manual gearbox. Every shift a fresh scream from an arm that's more open than closed — hand to the knob, muscle and bone arguing the whole way through every gear — and he drives. Blood filling the seat. Blood on the wheel. He drives to the next lot and gets out and the ground finally comes up to meet him, and a crowd gathers the way crowds do, too late and too loud, and a voice somewhere over him says the last thing he hears —

*You're losing—*

**FADE TO RED. THEN TO BLACK.**

### Note on placement
This is the bottom of the wound map — CUT 6 territory, "the origin told straight through, the boy that made the man." Every present-day cut in NOW should drop the reader toward *this.* The scalp, the spine, the shield-save, the drive: those are the beats the rest of the book keeps almost-touching and pulling back from, until here, at the deepest floor, it finally lets the whole night bleed out in one piece.
