# DARK SEVEN — THE LAST WITNESS
## Pencil-Ready Script · the closing bookend · the book's final pages
*Closes the loop **THE FIRST WITNESS** opened (pp. 1–5). The comedown after Cut 6's white. Quiet — give it air; fewer panels, longer holds. This is the exhale.*

> **RENDER — THE CLOSE.** Black, white, and grey. **No red anywhere.** The bleeding is over — the book spent all its red on the wound map, and that spending is the whole point. The one non-black element is **grace-(WHITE):** the warm light of the big house in the window. Structural payoff to state plainly: the book **opened** with the first red thrown onto this old face, and it **closes** with the last red gone and only the white of home. The Devil does not win.

## PAGE HH — THE MOVIE ENDS
*3 panels. The last frame lets him go.*

**PANEL 1.** The screen throws its final held **(WHITE)** — the shield up, the boy behind it — across the old soldier's face one last time. Render this white **warm,** grace, not the hard red-flicker that lit him all book. The old man lit white, and still.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He watched every frame. Red then grey then red. He never once looked away.

**PANEL 2.** The screen goes dark. The flicker stops. For the first time since page one, **no red reaches the room.** The old man in true, quiet dark. *(no caption — let it breathe.)*

**PANEL 3.** TIGHT on his face in the dark. He does the only thing a first witness — a last witness — can do.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He believes it. He receives it. He lets it be true and doesn't flinch it smaller.

## PAGE II — A MINUTE IN THE DARK
*3 panels. The body that carried a lot of weights, rising.*

**PANEL 1.** WIDE — the little room, black, the old man small in the recliner that knows the exact shape of him. He sits a minute. Doesn't move.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): And when it's over he sits a minute in the dark.

**PANEL 2.** TIGHT — his hand on the armrest. *(This is the hand that, back on page 3, tightened into the door handle of '96. At rest now.)* Draw it easy, open, done gripping.

**PANEL 3.** He gets up. Slow. The whole weight of a life in the rise.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Then he gets up slow. And crosses the little country of his room.

---

## PAGE JJ — THE WINDOW
*3 panels. Near, not crowding.*

**PANEL 1.** He reaches the window. Through it — the big house. The kitchen light. The boys' windows. Everyone still up, safe, winding down on their own screens. Render the big-house light as the one warm **(WHITE)** in the frame: grace, home, the far side of the door.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He looks out at the big house. Where they're all still up. Safe. Winding down.

**PANEL 2.** His reflection faint in the glass, laid over the warm windows of home — the old soldier and the house he guards, one image in one pane. Behind him, the recliner, empty and dark.

**PANEL 3.** And he knows, the way old soldiers know.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The shield held. The boy came back different — but came back.

## PAGE KK — THE LINE GOES ON
*2 panels. Close on the light.*

**PANEL 1 — near-full.** The old man at the window, small against the warm-white house — **near it, not crowding it,** the way he's stood his whole life. All the book's red is spent. What's left is black, and grey, and the one grace-white of home.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The line goes on.

**PANEL 2 — FINAL.** Hold on the window: the warm **(WHITE)** light, the old soldier's silhouette beside it. Fade — not to red, not to black. **Hold on the light of the house.**
> **HOLD ON THE BIG-HOUSE LIGHT. THE OLD SOLDIER NEAR IT, NOT CROWDING IT. FADE. THE DEVIL DOES NOT WIN.**
