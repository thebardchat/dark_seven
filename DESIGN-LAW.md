# DARK SEVEN — DESIGN LAW
*volume-three/DESIGN-LAW.md · the visual constitution · one source of truth*
*Every prompt file in this project obeys this. Recipes are thin — they name a center and point back here. If the law ever tightens, it tightens HERE, once.*

---

## ARTICLE I — THE THREE INKS *(absolute; break it nowhere)*

There are three inks and there is no fourth.

| Ink | Means | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **BLACK** | the world, and the dark | the default state of everything |
| **RED** | **blood, and blood only** | if it's red, it's bleeding. Nothing else is ever red. |
| **WHITE** | **grace** — the shield, the Light, rescue | reserved and rare. Earn it. |

**There is NO fourth color. Never green, gold, blue, cream, amber, or anything else.**

The one sanctioned extension: the **red neon Celtic cross** — grace *and* blood at once, burning in the dark. It is red because it is bought with blood. That is the only reason it is permitted to glow.

---

## ARTICLE II — THE COLOR-LOCK *(how the law survives contact with an image model)*

Image generators default to **warm tones.** They will drift the blood to brown/maroon/rust and the glow to cream/gold. Saying "blood red" is not enough — **you must name the wrong colors to push it off them.**

**The color-lock block — paste verbatim into every prompt:**

```
Colors are strictly limited to three and nothing else: pure black, clean bright
WHITE, and BOLD PURE BLOOD RED — a vivid, saturated crimson like fresh blood.
The blood must NOT be brown, maroon, rust, chocolate, or dark red.
Nothing may be cream, gold, amber, yellow, beige, green, or blue.
Everything that is not blood is pure high-contrast grayscale, including skin.
Absolutely no other colors anywhere in the frame.
```

If a generation comes back warm, re-paste this alone with: *"Keep everything exactly as it is. Change ONLY the colors."* It corrects without losing the take.

## ARTICLE III — THE CROSS IS NEVER REDRAWN

The Celtic cross is **Shane's actual tattoo**, worn across his whole back to cover the '96 scar down his spine. Grace laid over the wound. It is not decoration; it is the shield, made literal, on a real body.

- **Always attach the reference image.**
- **Always instruct: "do NOT redraw the cross."**
- Generating it from text alone **always** drifts to a generic cross. Never do it.
- On the page it appears as a **red neon cross burning in the black-and-white dark.**

## ARTICLE IV — THE VEIL *(law, and it overrides everything)*

- **Keystone** (the boy) is never named, never shown face-front, and his ordeal is **never depicted** — only felt, in symbol.
- **The Fractured Architect / the phantom** is only ever **a hand and a warm light.** Never a face. Never a body.
- **Draw around the subject, never at it.**
- **Tapioca's page is blank** — front and back, white, held open. His wounds are witnessed, never narrated. His story is his.

This law protects a real child and honors a real friend. If a request would break the veil, hold the line and refuse it.

## ARTICLE V — THE LOOP *(structure for any motion piece)*

Five beats, on **explicit timestamps.** Without them the model smears everything into one continuous bleed.

1. **IGNITE** — the center flickers on like a broken neon sign; stutters, fails to catch, sparks, a lightning arc, then surges to a steady glow.
2. **HOLD** — lit, steady, glowing.
3. **ENGULF** — thick glossy blood **COMPLETELY covers** the center until it is fully hidden. The glow dies.
   - **Blood direction depends on what the center is:**
     - **Falls from above** onto things that hang or stand — the cross, the shield, a numeral, a sign.
     - **Wells up from within** for things that *are the wound* — the knife that stays, a scar, a cut.
   - The blood always wins the frame by the end of this beat. Only its direction changes.
   - **The last thing the blood swallows should be the reason the blood is there** — the scars on the shield, the seventh tally, the welling at the blade.
4. **THE VOID** — the blood drains off the bottom in dripping strands while **pure black fills from the top**, until the frame is **100% SOLID BLACK and EMPTY.**
5. **REIGNITE** — the center flickers back to life, matching frame one. Seamless loop.

**Protect beat 4.** The empty black is the heartbeat of the whole thing — grace *gone*, before grace returns. Say **"COMPLETELY hidden"** and **"100% SOLID BLACK and EMPTY"** or the model will skip it.

**Sound:** low electrical hum + neon buzz under the flicker. Ask for it explicitly. It sells the mood.

## ARTICLE VI — THE HOUSE STYLE *(for stills / splash pages)*

```
Black-and-white world — the ONLY color anywhere in the image is blood red.
Glowing red neon Celtic crosses (flared arms, double ring, four-pointed star)
float throughout the scene. Blood drips from the top of the frame and pools
below. 1940s film-noir, heavy chiaroscuro, smoke and haze, sharp film grain,
dramatic cinematic splash-page composition. Bold, mythic, over-the-top.
```
Plus the **color-lock block** from Article II. Always.

---

## ARTICLE VII — MODULARITY: SWAP THE CENTER

The bones never change. **Only what's at the center does.**

Every teaser is: *ignite → hold → engulf → void → reignite*, around one central object. Swap that object and you have a new teaser in the same language. Approved centers:

- **The Celtic cross** — the shield / the tattoo *(the master teaser)*
- **The shield of scars** — five slashes + one buried knife, arranged as heraldry
- **The 7** — as seven tally-cuts (the seven who came); six white, the seventh bleeding
- **The knife that stays** — buried to the handle, welling red
- **A cut's key object** — one per chapter break

Same law, same five beats, same color-lock. That's how the whole book speaks one language.

**The blood covers the cross. The blood drains. The cross comes back.**

**The Devil does not win.**
