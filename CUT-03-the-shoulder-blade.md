# CUT 3 — THE SHOULDER BLADE
### NOW → the detective → deeper into the boy · the warm scratch that isn't a scratch
*Wound map, cut 3. Deeper than the spine. The blade that doesn't feel like a blade. Opens back to the man, then falls further into the boy than cut 2 dared. Black, white, and blood. Veil is law: nothing named, everything felt.*

---

**NOW. THE DESK. TWO WOUNDS OLD, ONE COMING.**

He's wearing both of them now, the arm and the spine, red seeping through two dressings, and he's still at the file because the file is the only thing in the room that matters and he's known that since it came under the door wearing his own face. **Arc** paces the gate behind him, and Arc is agitated in the specific way of a guard who's already failed once and hasn't forgiven himself.

*I should've stopped it,* Arc says. *It's my whole job to stop it. How does a thing get past me.*

The man could tell him. The man is about to learn it again, on his own body, the hard way.

**THE THIRD CUT**

This one he barely feels.

That's the horror of it — after the arm that screamed and the spine that ran him into the deep, the shoulder blade comes almost *gentle.* A hand pulls the coat aside and draws a line across the flat of his back and it lands like — nothing. Like a warm scratch. Like a fingernail. Like being *touched,* almost kindly, almost chosen.

The warm scratch that isn't a scratch.

He doesn't even reach for it. He's fought enough to know the ones that don't hurt are the ones already halfway to the bone, that *warm* is just the body's word for a cut so clean the nerves haven't caught up — but knowing it and feeling it are two different rooms, and his hand doesn't move to the wound because the wound feels like the opposite of a wound. Red spreads across his back, the only color in the grey, and it spreads *warm,* and some animal part of him wants to lean back into the thing that's opening him because it feels like a hand that means well.

**BACK TO THE DETECTIVE**

The blade finds the old shoulder-blade scar — of course it does — and drops him a half-second into the blown-out white:

*(— the fight, '96, the shirt yanked up, the warm line across the flat of his back mid-swing, the one cut in the whole massacre that didn't announce itself, the one he didn't know he'd taken until he saw the seat fill up later —)*

The cut that got the deepest reach in the whole war was the one that felt the least like a war. He learned that at fifteen. He forgot it, the way you forget the thing that would've saved you.

**DEEPER INTO THE BOY**

And then he doesn't come back to NOW. He goes *down,* past the room from before, past the false light and the blacked windows, down to a floor of the boy's dark he didn't reach last time — and down here, finally, he understands the thing that's been breaking him worst of all. The thing no father lets himself say out loud.

*The boy couldn't feel the blade. Because the blade came in warm.*

He feels it now from the inside, veiled and total, no face on it, no word for it: how the thing that opened his son didn't arrive as a knife at all. It arrived as *warmth.* As being seen. As being chosen. As *forever,* said early and often. It drew its line so gentle the nerves never caught up, and a good honest structure with all its doors unlocked leaned *back into it,* toward the hand it thought meant well, because a cut that feels like love is the only cut a true heart won't defend itself against.

And that — that is why the warnings bounced. Brave voices came from the edges. *This one is not what it says. This one is death.* And they hit a boy who could not feel the blade, only the warmth, and so they didn't sound like rescue. They sounded like someone trying to take away the one hand that felt kind. He didn't defend a villain. He defended a *warmth.* You cannot warn a body out of the wound that feels like being held.

The man falls through that and can't do anything, can't shout back up the years, can't put his own hand between his boy and the warm line being drawn down him — because it's already drawn, because the veil won't lift, because down here everything is felt and nothing can be reached.

**ARC, IN THE DARK**

*That's how it got past me,* Arc says, very quiet, from somewhere in the felt-black. *It didn't come at the gate like a threat. It came like something you'd want to let in. I open for warmth. I was built to open for warmth. That's the flaw it found. In you. In him. Same gate.*

**BACK UP**

He surfaces slower this time. Face over the desk. Three reds running now — arm, spine, shoulder — pooling on the nameless folder, warm going cold, his and the boy's indistinguishable, one color for two men cut by two hands thirty years apart with the exact same trick: *make it feel like anything but a blade.*

**BRACE**

Cut one: the blades have doors. Cut two: what's behind them is worse than his own history. Cut three: the deepest cuts are the ones that come in warm — and he is only halfway down.

At the edge of the frame the hand hasn't moved, and now he understands why it's never had to reach. It doesn't grab. It *offers.* Ringless, open, patient, warm-looking. Waiting exactly where something was already going to swim, holding out the one thing no honest heart is armored against.

*Three,* the dark says, gentle as a scratch. *Halfway. The warm ones are behind you now. The rest just hurt.*

**HOLD ON THE THREE REDS. FADE.**

### Note on placement
Cut 3 of the wound map — the "warm scratch that isn't a scratch" carries the book's cruelest truth: the deepest wound is the one that doesn't register as a wound. It maps '96 (the shoulder-blade cut the detective didn't feel until he saw the seat fill) onto the boy (grooming that arrived as warmth, which is why he defended it and the warnings bounced). The veil stays total — the predation is rendered only as *warmth-that-cuts,* no fact, no name, no act. Arc's confession completes the crew-as-mind seam from cut 1: the gate opens for warmth by design, in the man and in the boy — the same gate, the same flaw, the thing the dark found in both. Escalation locked: cut 3 is deeper than cut 2, and the man is told he's only halfway.
