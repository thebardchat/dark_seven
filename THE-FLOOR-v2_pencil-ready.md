# DARK SEVEN — THE FLOOR v2
## Pencil-Ready Script · *'96, The Origin — Told Straight, From the Full Ramble*
*volume-three/script/ · REPLACES THE FLOOR v1 · pressed from Shane's complete telling · DRAFT — confirm keepers*
*This is where Cut 6's key-turn lands. Told straight through — no cuts back, no veil. The one sequence with no timeline-jumping: the origin, whole.*

> **RENDER.** Blown-out **daylight-white**, thin black line, hard shadow — a warm May afternoon overexposed to near-white. **Blood is the only color, always.** `(RED)` marks it.
> **THE CLOCK.** Shane flagged the time as significant. Put it **on the page** — small, corner-stamped, like evidence: `3:45 PM`.
> **THE HORNS.** From the first blade to the scatter, horns thread the gutters — `HOOONK` — several, not just one or two, rising and dopplering off. They never stop for any of it.
> **STAGING NOTE.** This version follows the ramble where it differs from the first telling: Tapioca drives; Shane rides passenger; the Mustang brakes AHEAD while the Cadillac pins BEHIND; the spine cut lands mid-mount; the save comes over the HOOD. Earlier staging superseded. Flagged at the end.

## PAGE F1 — 3:45 PM · THE TERMS
*3 panels. The handshake before the world changes.*

**PANEL 1.** Blown white. A driveway, a father's hands lowering **two cases of beer** into a car. Corner stamp: `3:45 PM · PROM NIGHT`.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Prom night. My father puts two cases of beer in the vehicle.

**PANEL 2.** The father, spare, archetype not portrait — laying down the rule. The boys listening.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The rule was strict. Straight to the hotel room off the drive. Don't touch it until we were safe and in for the night.

**PANEL 3.** A nod between men. Done deal.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **We agreed to his terms.**

---

## PAGE F2 — THE CIVIC
*3 panels. A kid's car, about to become evidence.*

**PANEL 1.** INT. — **Tapioca driving** his little manual Honda Civic. Shane shotgun, right arm out the open window. Warm May light everywhere. Windows down.

**PANEL 2.** The floorboard truth: papers pushed around, **cereal bowls and spoons** — breakfast eaten on morning drives to school. A seventeen-year-old's car.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Cereal bowls and spoons from where he ate breakfast driving to school. That kind of car. That kind of life.

**PANEL 3.** WIDE — the six lanes, three each way, the turn lane down the middle. Two kids on the parkway headed for the drive, doing exactly what a father asked.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A beautiful day in May.

---

## PAGE F3 — THE MUSTANG
*4 panels. Less than a glance, taken wrong.*

**PANEL 1.** A **fox-body Mustang** pulls alongside — off-color, music up loud enough to turn a head.

**PANEL 2.** The glance: a **woman driving.** A man low in the passenger seat Shane doesn't see at first.

**PANEL 3.** She looks over. Says something to the man — describing. His head turns.

**PANEL 4.** The man **flips the bird first.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Of course a seventeen-year-old is going to flip it back. So I did.

---

## PAGE F4 — THE BLEACHER SEAT
*3 panels. The tell nobody clocks in time.*

**PANEL 1.** The passenger hauls himself **out onto the window sill like a bleacher seat**, half out of a moving car, bucking, yelling.

**PANEL 2.** He hurls things at the boy's face — one, another, another.
> SFX: *BAP* *BAP*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Missing every opportunity.**

**PANEL 3.** Then the wave — **back to front** — like waving something forward. Not at them. *Behind* them.

---

## PAGE F5 — THE CADILLAC · THE REVOLVER
*4 panels. The hunt shows its teeth, and one tooth gets put away.*

**PANEL 1.** The boy turns to look back. WIDE: a **long stretched Cadillac** coming up — **six bodies. Six men.**

**PANEL 2.** TIGHT, through their windshield: one of them **slaps what looks like a revolver on the dash.**

**PANEL 3.** Another's hands **slap his** — makes him put it away. Down. Gone.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): One of them slaps a revolver on the dash. Hands slap it away.

**PANEL 4.** The two cars breathing around the Civic now, front and back. *(Let the image carry what almost happened. No caption.)*

---

## PAGE F6 — THE BOX
*3 panels. Trapped at the turn they never make.*

**PANEL 1.** Far right lane. The turn coming up — the hotel is almost *right there.*

**PANEL 2.** The **Cadillac closes to the bumper** — as close as steel gets.

**PANEL 3.** The **Mustang stands on its brakes** dead ahead.
> SFX: **SKREEEE**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Boxed. At the turn we never made.

---

## PAGE F7 — CUT 1 · THE ARM
*3 panels. First blood. The horns begin and never stop.*

**PANEL 1.** EXTREME TIGHT — the boy's right arm, hanging out the open passenger window, easy, seventeen.

**PANEL 2 — BIG.** A blade through it. Muscle. The deep cold wrongness of bone. **FIRST (RED)** — the first color in the world, sheeting off the elbow, onto the door, into the white.
> SFX: **SHNK**

**PANEL 3.** And the horns start.
> SFX *(gutters, from here to the scatter)*: **HOOONK · HOOONK · HOOONK**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The horns will not stop for any of it.

---

## PAGE F8 — THE CODE WORD
*3 panels. The name that means we fight together.*

**PANEL 1.** Tapioca, wild-eyed at the wheel:
> TAPIOCA: **GET OUT.**

**PANEL 2.** And then he yells it — the word. Letter it big, alone on the panel:
> TAPIOCA: **"TAPIOCA!"**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The code word. It meant: out — separate ways — and we fight.

**PANEL 3.** WIDE. Two doors flung open. Two boys stepping out into six screaming lanes, each his own way, into it.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): So we followed orders. Ours this time.

*(PRESSED READING, confirm: the friend is called Tapioca in these books BECAUSE this was the word — the name and the battle cry are the same thing. "Naming is an act of rescue" — this is why. Your call to lock.)*

## PAGE F9 — THE FIRST TWO
*4 panels. The only math he knows.*

**PANEL 1.** Something **cracks him upside the head** — held in a fist, hard.
> SFX: **KRAK**

**PANEL 2.** He grabs *whatever it is they're holding* — doesn't matter what — and the man attached to it.

**PANEL 3.** Picks him clean up. **Slams him to the road.**

**PANEL 4.** Knee down. Hand on the throat. **Squeezing the life out.** Heels drumming asphalt.
> SFX: *kh—kh—kh*

---

## PAGE F10 — THE SWARM
*2 panels. His friend, going under. Letter the caption in Shane's doubled rhythm — exactly.*

**PANEL 1 — WIDE.** What he sees when he looks up: they are **on Tapioca.** All of them at once.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Cutting and stabbing and hitting and holding. And cutting and stabbing and hitting and holding.**

**PANEL 2.** He lets the sure thing go — the choke released, the man forgotten — and runs to his friend.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I let go of a won fight to get to him. The only math I've ever known.

---

## PAGE F11 — THE SLAM
*3 panels. Hit blind, answer bigger.*

**PANEL 1.** The instant he's moving — **hit from the side.** Blind.
> SFX: **WHUMP**

**PANEL 2.** He gets under the new one. **Up and over the shoulder** — a fireman's lift in the middle of a highway.

**PANEL 3 — BIG.** **SLAMS him down between the cars.** Steel on both sides, road underneath.

---

## PAGE F12 — THE MOUNT
*3 panels. Committed — and open.*

**PANEL 1.** He mounts the man. The onslaught begins.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Pulverizing. Blood going everywhere. Every hit, more blood.

**PANEL 2.** Fists falling like weather. **(RED)** spattering up the white.

**PANEL 3.** And behind him — unseen — a hand takes the hem of his shirt.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I couldn't stop hitting long enough to guard my own back.

---

## PAGE F13 — CUTS 2 & 3 · THE SPINE, THE SHOULDER BLADE
*4 panels. The binding of the book, drawn at full length. And the blade that missed his name.*

**PANEL 1.** The shirt yanked up, his back bared — mid-mount, mid-swing.

**PANEL 2 — TALL.** The line drawn **from the top of the spine all the way down.** Slow. The whole length. **(RED)** welling behind the blade's travel.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **It feels more like a scratch. Like a person was scratching to get me.**

**PANEL 3.** Another line — across the **shoulder blade.** TIGHT on where it lands: beside a small tattoo. His first. A nickname — three, four letters. *(The word itself never shown. It's his.)*

**PANEL 4.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): They were aiming for the tattoo. My first one. My name.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **They missed it.**

---

## PAGE F14 — CUT 4 · THE SKULL
*3 panels. Where the night was always driving.*

**PANEL 1.** A tingle at the back of the skull — like scratching. He almost doesn't register it.

**PANEL 2.** Then a fist takes **as much hair as it can hold** and *hauls.* The head back. The **neck exposed, bared to the white sky.**

**PANEL 3.** Behind him, unseen: a man **reaching up to take a full swing** — the cut meant to open his throat.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Unknowingly to me.

---

## PAGE F15 — OVER THE HOOD · THE SAVE
*FULL-BLEED SPLASH. The image the whole trilogy is built on.*

**SINGLE IMAGE.** **TAPIOCA comes over the HOOD of a car** — airborne, already torn, an ear half off and pouring **(RED)**, stabbed, cut, spending a body he has no business still standing in — and lands his fist **in the man's temple.** Lights out. **The swing never finishes.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He jumps the hood and hits him in the temple and the man is out before the blade is.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The finish doesn't have to land. Somebody can come over the car.

---

## PAGE F16 — ALL RED
*3 panels. The curtain comes down over his eyes — and he keeps swinging.*

**PANEL 1.** The blood that was leaking out of his skull **rushes over his face.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Everything turned red.**

**PANEL 2.** Through the red: fists still falling. His. More punches. More fury.

**PANEL 3.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): It did not slow down the onslaught.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **This was fight or flight, and no way was I flying **** anywhere.**

---

## PAGE F17 — THE BRAKES · THE SCATTER
*3 panels. The spell breaks.*

**PANEL 1.** Somewhere in the stopped lanes, a driver **stands on the brakes** — long, screaming.
> SFX: **SKREEEEEEE**

**PANEL 2.** The horns swell — loud for a second — then **doppler off into the distance. Several of them. Not just one or two.**

**PANEL 3 — WIDE.** And the **two carloads take off** — Mustang and Cadillac swallowed back into the traffic they came out of. Gone.

---

## PAGE F18 — THE DRIVER'S SEAT
*4 panels. The part nobody would believe if it weren't true.*

**PANEL 1.** He gets into the **driver's seat of Tapioca's car.** *(The switch: the wounded one drives his friend's Civic out.)*

**PANEL 2 — EXTREME TIGHT.** He looks down at his forearm.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **I could see the bone and the muscle.**

**PANEL 3.** The manual gearbox. His ruined arm arguing with the shifter through every gear.

**PANEL 4.** The next parking lot. The Civic stopping crooked. The door.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I made it into the next parking lot. And that's when I began to fade.

---

## PAGE F19 — THE GROUND
*3 panels. The bottom of the whole book. Cut 6 rises from this black.*

**PANEL 1.** He's down — **sitting on the ground, looking up.**

**PANEL 2 — the image.** Above him: **his buddy. Standing.** The ear hanging off. Stabbed. Cut. Still on his feet, over his friend.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I remember sitting on the ground looking up at my buddy's ear hanging off. He'd been stabbed. Cut. And he was standing.

**PANEL 3.** The inventory, going soft at the edges: the opening in the back. The shoulder blade. The head. The forearm, open to the bone.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I could feel every opening I owned.
> **FADE TO (RED). THEN TO BLACK. → Cut 6, Page R: he rises.**
