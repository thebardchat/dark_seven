# TAPIOCA — "I See You, Brother"
*volume-three/ · pressed from Shane's drive · DRAFT — confirm keepers before locking*
*THE MAN, to the one who came over the car. Pressed from the voice, nothing added. His story stays his.*

## THE ADDRESS *(shaped from the dump)*

The scars. **I see them, and I recognize them. I always have and I always will.**

More than the physical scar. More than the ear that was dangling off. More than the knife left in your back, the cut around your waist — all the way around. The cut around your thigh — all the way around. The tricep. More than what they did to your body on that highway.

**It's the wound that was left behind. The wound you carry.**

For the longest time I didn't know you carried it. You were always somewhere else — adventures, traveling, the jungle, everywhere you could imagine. World traveler. Always. Happy, strong, like you had it all together.

So I pushed myself to be like you. And every time I fell short, I went further into my own wounds — because I was nowhere near what your outside was showing me.

That's all I'll say about that. The rest is yours. Your scars, your story, your version — and it's yours to tell, not mine. I'll leave the space open.

**I see you, brother. I see you.**

And know that I see you. I always think about you — even when I go quiet. I'm not the easiest friend to have. I can go without calling, without the checkups. **I can hide in my own shelter, my own world. And it was never anything intentional.**

**I see. I see.**

## THE PRINCIPLE — the Tapioca space stays open
Same law as the veil around Keystone: **there are wounds that aren't yours to narrate.** You witness them, you salute them, you love him out loud — but you do NOT tell the story of the load he carries inside. That's his to tell, in his own voice, if he ever chooses to. The book draws around it and leaves the door open. Reserved. Respected.
