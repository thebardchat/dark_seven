# DARK SEVEN — COVER + SIX CUT TITLE CARDS
## Design Spec · the face of the book & the six chapter breaks
*Three inks only: BLACK (the world/the dark) · RED (blood, the one color) · WHITE (grace, reserved). The physical spine of the book is the scar. Red accumulates card to card; white breaks in at Cut 5. The Devil does not win.*

---

# PART A — THE COVER

## THE BIG IDEA
**The scar is the binding.** Design the wrap — back, spine, front — as **one continuous image,** built so the long box-cutter scar down the man's spine falls exactly on the *physical spine of the book.* On a shelf, DARK SEVEN is a black book with a single **(RED)** wound running top to bottom of its spine. The thing that holds the pages together is the wound that made the man.

## THE WRAP *(one image across back–spine–front)*
- **Subject:** the man's bare back, seen from behind, heavy black chiaroscuro, most of him swallowed in shadow. The long **(RED)** scar runs vertically down his spine — placed dead-center so it lands on the book's spine.
- **The six cuts branch off it** at their sites: the arm, the spine (the line itself), the shoulder blade, the scalp/nape, the neck, and low in the back — the one that isn't a slash but a buried **knife,** handle standing out, welling red. Six wounds, one body, one binding.
- Everything else black and white. The scars are the *only* color on the whole wrap.

## FRONT COVER
- **Title: `DARK SEVEN`** — big, stark, hand-cut lettering, like a box-cutter dragged through white. Set in white on black, with the scar cresting over the spine to bleed one **(RED)** drip onto the front.
- **The 7** worked as **seven tally-cuts** (the seven who came) — six inked white, the seventh dripping red.
- **Subtitle:** *Black & White & Red All Over.*
- **Series line:** *You Probably Think This Book Is About You · Volume Three · The Culmination.*
- **Byline:** [ — your byline, exactly as it reads on Books 1 & 2 — ]

## SPINE *(the literal wound)*
- Black field. The **(RED)** scar-line at full height, continuous with the wrap.
- `DARK SEVEN` · `VOL. III` · byline — white, small, set beside the red line, never on top of it.
- Shelf read: a black spine with one red wound down it. Unmistakable at ten feet.

## BACK COVER
- Black, sparse. The **shield-made-of-scars** motif small and centered (the five slashes + the buried knife arranged as heraldry, red on black). At the bottom edge — the **hand, withdrawing** into the black, its warm light guttering (ties to the last page). Barcode block kept clean in a white chip.
- **Cover copy (veil-safe logline draft):**
  > In a black-and-white world where the only color is blood, a man takes the one case he was never meant to work — because it's really about the thing that came for his son. Every wound he takes reopens an older one. Six cuts, each deeper than the last, down to a highway thirty years gone. One shield. One knife that never came out. *The Devil does not win.*

## TYPE + PRODUCTION
- **Type:** condensed, brutal display for the title with a scratched/hand-cut texture; clean quiet sans for copy. Never decorative — the letters should look *cut,* not drawn.
- **Finish:** matte black stock with **spot-gloss or spot-red foil on the blood only.** Physical red that catches the light when the book turns in a hand — so even in the reader's grip, the only thing that shines is what bleeds.

# PART B — THE SIX CUT TITLE CARDS

## UNIFYING DEVICE *(all six cards)*
- **Full black page.** A single **(RED)** vertical line — the spine, the binding — runs down every card. Each cut **branches off that line** at its body-site, so the six cards together trace the wound map onto one body.
- **The cut number + name** in cut-white type.
- **A small "scars left" counter** in a corner, ticking **6 → 0** across the book — the countdown made visible.
- **The dark's voice** lettered small in its torn, red-dripping box, counting the cuts down (its through-line, carried onto the breaks).
- **Progression is the point:** card 1 is nearly all black-and-white, one clean line; **red accumulates** card to card until card 4 is half-drowned; **white breaks in at card 5;** card 6 hands off to the held white. The cards *feel* the descent even standing alone.

---

### CARD 1 — CUT 1 · THE ARM
- **Image:** the red spine-line, and **one clean red slash** high on it (the arm). Singular. The most black-and-white of the six — the shallowest cut.
- **Counter:** *scars left: 5*
- **THE DARK:** *This is the shallow one. This is the one that barely hurt.*

### CARD 2 — CUT 2 · DOWN THE SPINE
- **Image:** the wound **is** the binding — draw the red spine-line thick and full-height, a box-cutter drawing down it, red running into the actual gutter of the spread. This card and the book's spine are one object.
- **Counter:** *scars left: 4*
- **THE DARK:** *That was the second one. You've got four scars left. You know exactly how deep they go.*

### CARD 3 — CUT 3 · THE SHOULDER BLADE
- **Image:** spine-line + a **thin, almost tender** red line branching across the shoulder — the warm scratch, drawn gentle, the one that doesn't announce itself.
- **Counter:** *scars left: 3*
- **THE DARK:** *Three. Halfway. The warm ones are behind you now. The rest just hurt.*

### CARD 4 — CUT 4 · THE SCALP
- **Image:** spine-line + red at the **crown-to-nape,** and the card itself starting to **tip toward inverted** — red drowning more of the black than any card yet. The darkest, deepest break.
- **Counter:** *scars left: 2*
- **THE DARK:** *Two. This is the bottom. There's nothing under you but the last cut.*
- **[PRINT NOTE:** this is the card that cues the flip — the book turns upside down on the page-turn into Cut 4.]

### CARD 5 — CUT 5 · THE NECK / THE SHIELD
- **Image:** spine-line + the neck mark — but for the first time, **(WHITE)** blazes in behind and through the red. Grace breaking the black. The turn made visible.
- **Counter:** *scars left: 1*
- **THE MAN** *(the dark goes quiet here — his voice takes the card):* **The finish doesn't have to land.**

### CARD 6 — CUT 6 · THE KNIFE THAT STAYS
- **Image:** spine-line + a single **buried knife** low on the back — not a slash, a handle standing out, red welling. The **shield-made-of-scars** ghosted faint behind it. **(WHITE)** swelling at the edges — the fade-to-white beginning.
- **Counter:** *scars left: 0*
- **THE MAN:** **The wound made the wall.** *→ then white.*

---

## BOOKEND CARDS *(optional, to frame the frame)*
- **THE FIRST WITNESS** — opening card. Black. The old soldier's silhouette in the recliner, the screen's first **(RED)** just touching his face. Sets the frame.
- **THE LAST WITNESS** — closing card. Black. The same silhouette at the window, no red left anywhere — only the warm **(WHITE)** of the big house. Closes it.

---
*Cover + breaks specced. On a shelf: a black book bleeding down its spine. Inside: six black cards, red deepening to a drowning, then white. Next and last: **THE LAST WITNESS** — the closing bookend, penciled — the old soldier lowering the final frame, the loop closed.*
