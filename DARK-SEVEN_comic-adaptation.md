# PART I — THE ART BIBLE
*The "spin," codified. This is the law an artist letters, inks, and lays out to. Read once, obey always.*

## THE THREE INKS
This book has three inks and no fourth. Every page is built from exactly these:

- **BLACK** — the world. The rain, the grey city, the dark, the phantom's element. Heavy chiaroscuro, deep ink-wash, high contrast. When in doubt, more black.
- **RED (spot color) — blood, and blood only.** The single hue in creation. Never a red coat, red sign, red rose, red taillight. If it is red, it is bleeding. Break this nowhere.
- **WHITE — grace.** Reserved and rare. The shield. The Light on the hill. The locked door that's a real mercy. Rescue. Used so sparingly that when a page goes white it lands like the first breath after drowning. White is *earned,* never default background.

The reader's eye is trained across the book: **black is what the dark does, red is what it costs, white is what saves.**

## THE SPINE IS THE GUTTER  *(the signature device)*
The scar down his back is the binding of the book — so make it literally so. On two-page spreads, the **center gutter is the spine.** 
- In **Cut 2 (Down the Spine),** the box-cutter line is inked *down the actual center gutter,* red bleeding into the seam, so the wound and the book's spine are one object in the reader's hands.
- Recurring: a thin vertical red line can live in the gutter of key spreads — the scar, always there, holding the pages on.

## THREE TIMELINES, TOLD BY RENDER — NOT BY HUE
Color law is absolute, so timelines are distinguished by *how they're drawn,* never by adding color:

- **NOW** — rain-soaked, heaviest blacks, tight panels, wet reflective surfaces. The grey present.
- **'96 (THE FLOOR)** — **high-key, overexposed white.** A bright day blown out to near-white, thin black line, hard shadows. *(LOCKED 2026-07-08 — Shane's call: color law is absolute. '96 is blinding daylight-white, never gold. Blood stays the only color even here, and reads brightest against the white.)*
- **THE VEIL (the boy's dark)** — **inverted: white line on solid black.** Negative space. The felt-dark. Almost no detail; symbol only. This is where we draw *around* the subject, never at it.

The blood-red is the one thread that crosses all three. When a blade in NOW finds an old scar and the page drops, the **transition is a red slash used as the panel gutter** — the cut becomes the border.

## THE VEIL GRAMMAR  *(how we protect Keystone on the page)*
The boy's ordeal is felt, never shown. No name, no face, no fact — in art that means:
- **The phantom / Fractured Architect is NEVER fully in frame.** Only: a hand (ringless, open, patient), a shadow with no owner, a "warm" light source from off-panel. Never a face. Never a body. It is a *hand and a light,* nothing more.
- **Keystone is never face-front.** Back of the head, silhouette, a small figure dwarfed by vast architecture. We see his world (the false-moon bulb, the blacking windows, the inverted cathedral) — never the act, never his face in the act.
- **Everything symbol.** A bulb lying about being the moon. Windows going black one at a time under a patient brush. Grey chemical weather smothering a true sky. A cathedral upside down. The reader feels the pit in the chest and is handed zero facts. That is the horror and the mercy both.

## THE CREW = THE MIND  *(Arc / Weld)*
The Mega Bots are facets of one man — draw them so:
- **Same silhouette as the man,** rendered as shadow-selves / reflections that step out of him. Not separate people — pieces.
- **Arc** = the gate/threshold figure (a warden's stance, always at an edge or doorway). **Weld** = the hand/fixer (defined by the hands — a maker's hands).
- The gut-punch is visual: when the room "reassembles into a shield" (Cut 5), it's a **splash of every fragment of him turning to face the same direction** — the crew revealed as one man, all of him a wall.

## THE CAPTION BOXES  *(two voices)*
- **THE MAN** — his interior. Clean black box, white type, terse noir. Present tense.
- **THE DARK** — the phantom's voice. **White type on black, box edge torn and dripping red at the bottom corner.** Quiet, almost kind, never shouting. It counts the cuts down: *"That was the second one. Four scars left."*
- No omniscient narrator. Comics show. Captions are only these two voices.

## THE GRAVITY FLIP  *(Cut 4 → Cut 5, the structural climax)*
- **Cut 4 (The Scalp) prints upside down.** As the world inverts on the page, the panels invert with it — the reader physically **rotates the book** to read the bottom of the descent. This is the deepest floor; the reader's own hands go upside down to reach it.
- **Cut 5 (The Shield) is where it rights.** The instant the man "stops being the neck and becomes the shield," the page — and the book in the reader's hands — turns right-side up again. Gravity is a thing the reader *does.* The turn of the book is the turn of the man.

## PAGE ARCHITECTURE — THE DESCENT IS BUILT INTO THE RHYTHM
Each cut goes deeper; make the pages feel it:
- Panel counts loosen and gutters **widen** cut over cut — the page physically opens up / falls as we descend.
- Red **accumulates** down the book: Cut 1 is one red thread; by Cut 4 the page can be half-drowned; Cut 5/6 pull it back so white can break through.
- Recurring refrain panels: **the hand at the edge of the frame** (small, patient, unmoving) closes many scenes. And **the reds running together on the nameless folder** — his blood and the boy's, indistinguishable — is a returning image, one color for two men.

## LOCKED CODE-NAMES  *(never a real name on the page)*
- **Keystone** = the boy. The crown-stone of the arch; the Architect tried to pull it, it held. Load-bearing, never a victim.
- **The Fractured Architect / the phantom** = the predator. A hand and a light. Never a face.
- **Tapioca** = the '96 friend who came over the car.
- **the man** = the detective. Nameless.

## OPENING FRAME — THE FIRST WITNESS
*The old soldier watches it first. The book can open and close here. In this room the only red is the blood of the movie itself, thrown on an old man's face by the screen.*

**PAGE 1 — full-bleed splash.**
The apartment on the side of the house, black on black. One recliner. One window, and through it — small, warm, far — the big house's kitchen light and the boys' windows. The old man in the chair, rendered in grey, holding himself the way men hold a weight they never set down out loud. No red anywhere yet. The whole world drained.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): There's a door on the side of the house that goes to its own small country.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Near the ones he'd die for. Not underfoot. He spent a life learning the difference between guarding a thing and crowding it.

**PAGE 2 — 3 tall panels.**
P1. The screen comes on, off-panel; its light hits the wall. Black-and-white pours across the recliner. P2. Tight on the old face, lit grey by the screen. P3. The *first cut on screen* throws the first **(RED)** across his face — the only color in the room. Red on the old man. Then grey again.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He has seen what a wound is. Not on a screen. In the field. On boys younger than the one who made this movie.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): If one man on earth is qualified to be first to watch a story told entirely in scars — it's him.
> SFX (soft, from the screen): —*the horns, far off*—

**PAGE 3 — 4 panels, intercut: the recliner NOW / the errand THEN.**
The old man's hand tightens on the armrest — MATCH CUT to a younger hand on a door handle, thirty years back. '96 rendered in blown white for one panel, then back to the grey room.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(torn box, dripping)* You handed him a chore on a bright day.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *Take this to the room. Don't touch it till you're there.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): No father knows, when he sends his boy out the door, which afternoons come back different.

**PAGE 4 — the line of three men. Wide, three figures, one doorway repeated.**
A grandfather in a door. A son in a door. A boy — Keystone, silhouette only — in a door, stepping into hard clean **(WHITE)** light. The wound and the strength coming down the line as one red thread.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Three men. One door, over and over. He couldn't shield his own. The boy's in the light now because the shield finally went up.

**PAGE 5 — close, and hold.**
The old face, red-then-grey. He does not look away. Final panel: he believes it. Behind him, the big-house light steady in the window.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He does the only thing a first witness can do. He believes it. He receives it. He lets it be true and doesn't flinch it smaller.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *I saw what happened to you. I saw what you did about it. I believe every cut.*
> **FADE. Hold on the old face — (RED), then grey. The big-house light in the window.**

## CUT 1 — THE ARM
*NOW · the frame · first blood · teaches the reader to brace.*

**PAGE 6 — establishing, rain, no color in it.**
The city black-and-white through a filthy window. The man in it. Note the right arm, held a little away from himself. One-panel intro of **Arc** as a shadow-self at the threshold of the room.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He runs a crew. That's what he tells himself. Arc keeps the gate. Nothing reaches him Arc hasn't cleared first.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): So when the case slides under the door — Arc's supposed to stop it.

**PAGE 7 — the case + the hand.**
The nameless file under the door. He knows its weight and doesn't open it. Then — bottom corner of the page, small, where the streetlight gives out — **the hand.** Ringless. Patient. It grew its own little light and hung it out front. It does not reach.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Some cases you don't investigate. Some you recognize.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *Nothing to do with him.* That's what the surface says. Everything to do with him.
> CAPTION (ARC): I can't hold this one. It's already inside. It came in wearing your own face.

**PAGE 8 — the ambush snaps shut. 5 panels, tightening.**
Shape out of the grey → the shape has a hand → the hand has a line of light → before his brain files it as *blade* — it's through the sleeve and through the arm.
> SFX: *SHNK*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Thirty years running crews and I don't hear the room change until the room's already closed.

**PAGE 9 — FIRST BLOOD. The reveal of the color law.**
Big panel. The first **(RED)** comes up out of him — red on a grey floor, red on a grey coat, one bright drop then the string of them. The single hot wrong note in a black-and-white world.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(dripping box)* This is the shallow one. This is the one that barely hurt.

**PAGE 10 — the scar underneath. The first drop-through.**
The blade doesn't find new skin — it finds the **old line.** The steel touches the thirty-year scar and the scar *answers.* Panel border becomes a **red slash;** through it, one blown-white **'96** fragment: warm day, windows down, a woman driving, the bird returning over the door. Then he slams the door on it — back to grey, bleeding fresh over old.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The blade doesn't find new skin. It finds the old line.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Every cut from here is going to find an old one. Every blade in this book has a door in it. And they only go *down.*

**PAGE 11 — brace. The red thumbprint.**
He picks up the file with the arm that still works. Leaves a **(RED)** thumbprint on the nameless grey folder. At the edge of the frame the hand hasn't moved.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *That was the shallow one.*
> **Hold on the red thumbprint. FADE.**

## CUT 2 — DOWN THE SPINE
*NOW → the first descent · the book's own binding · the veil goes up.*
**→ SCRIPT NOTE: this entire sequence is a TWO-PAGE SPREAD built on the center gutter. The box-cutter line runs down the actual seam of the book.**

**PAGES 12–13 — THE SPINE SPREAD.**
Left page: the desk, NOW, heavier rain. Arm wrapped, still bleeding a slow red dot. **Weld** at his shoulder — a maker's hands, flexing, wanting in.
> CAPTION (WELD): Point me at it. Whatever did this. I'll make it right. That's what I'm for.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Some of this is already done. You can't weld a door that closed thirty years ago.

Across the gutter: the hand fists the back of his coat, hauls it up, bares the spine. The blade draws — not a stab, a *drawing* — a slow bright **(RED)** box-cutter line **down the center gutter of the spread,** vertebra by vertebra. The wound and the book's spine are one object.
> SFX: *(no sound — just the warm coming up before the pain files its report)*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The crease the whole book is folded along.

**PAGES 14–15 — the veil goes up.**
He expects '96. He doesn't get '96. He falls through his own oldest scar into a dark that isn't his. **Render flips to white-line-on-black.** Symbol only, no face, no edges: a bulb where a moon should hang, lying about being the moon. Windows going black one at a time under a patient brush. Grey chemical weather smothering a true sky. Rest outlawed — *not allowed to close, wake up.* A cathedral learning the red on its own floor is nothing to cry about. **We see none of it clearly. It is all felt.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Nothing here has edges. Nothing here has a name.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): This is the why. You wanted to know why every cut in your body still bleeds. You've been bleeding down toward this the whole time.

**PAGE 16 — Weld, useless for the first time.**
Down in the felt-dark, Weld's maker's hands reach for a seam to close, a bolt to set — and find no metal. Nothing to grip. The hands that never failed open and close on smoke. Something in Weld that never broke makes a sound going down.
> CAPTION (WELD): There's no edge to weld. You cannot repair a wound that happened to someone else in a room you weren't allowed into.

**PAGE 17 — back up. Two reds run together.**
He claws out with one working arm, breaks the surface into NOW, face down over the desk. The red from the arm and the red from the spine pool on the nameless folder until **you can't tell the two wounds apart.** His and the boy's. The same red.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): That was the second one. You've got four scars left. You know exactly how deep they go.
> **Hold on the two reds running together. FADE.**

## CUT 3 — THE SHOULDER BLADE
*NOW → the detective → deeper into the boy · the warm scratch that isn't a scratch.*

**PAGE 18 — the desk, two wounds old, one coming.** Arc paces the gate — a guard who's failed once and won't forgive himself.
> CAPTION (ARC): I should've stopped it. It's my whole job to stop it. How does a thing get past me.

**PAGE 19 — the third cut. The horror of gentleness.**
A hand pulls the coat aside and draws a line across the flat of his back — and it lands like *nothing.* Like a warm scratch. Like being touched, almost kindly. **(RED)** spreads warm, and some animal part of him wants to lean back into the thing opening him.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The ones that don't hurt are the ones already halfway to the bone. *Warm* is just the body's word for a cut so clean the nerves haven't caught up.

**PAGE 20 — the detective's flash.** Red-slash border → blown-white '96: the shirt yanked up, the warm line across the flat of his back mid-swing, the one cut in the whole massacre that didn't announce itself.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The cut that got the deepest reach in the whole war was the one that felt the least like a war. I learned that at fifteen. I forgot it.

**PAGES 21–22 — deeper into the boy. The center of the book's grief. (Veil render.)**
He doesn't come back. He goes down to a floor of the boy's dark he didn't reach before, and understands the thing no father lets himself say: *the boy couldn't feel the blade — because the blade came in warm.* Rendered in symbol only, no face, no fact: the thing that opened his son didn't arrive as a knife. It arrived as warmth. As being seen. As being chosen. As *forever,* said early and often. A good honest structure, all its doors unlocked because it never learned it needed locks, leaning *back into* the hand it thought meant well.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A cut that feels like love is the only cut a true heart won't defend itself against.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Brave voices came from the edges. *This one is not what it says. This one is death.* And they hit a boy who could only feel the warmth — so they sounded like someone trying to take away the one hand that felt kind.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): You cannot warn a body out of the wound that feels like being held.

**PAGE 23 — Arc understands the flaw.**
> CAPTION (ARC): It didn't come at the gate like a threat. It came like something you'd want to let in. I open for warmth. I was built to open for warmth. That's the flaw it found. In you. In him. Same gate.

**PAGE 24 — back up. Three reds now.**
Face over the desk. Arm, spine, shoulder — three reds pooling, warm going cold, his and the boy's indistinguishable. The hand at the edge of the frame hasn't moved. Now he sees why it never had to reach: **it doesn't grab. It offers.**
> CAPTION (THE DARK): Three. Halfway. The warm ones are behind you now. The rest just hurt.
> **Hold on the three reds. FADE.**

## CUT 4 — THE SCALP
*NOW (the huge fight) — the deepest dive · crown to nape · the world goes upside down.*
**→ SCRIPT NOTE: FROM PAGE 27 ON, THE BOOK PRINTS UPSIDE DOWN. The reader rotates it to descend. Panels invert with the world.**

**PAGE 25 — no desk anymore. All war.**
The file stopped being paper and became a fight. No office, no window — only bodies in the grey and the man throwing the arm that still works. **The whole crew is up:** Arc screaming at a gate off its hinges, Weld swinging and connecting with half of it — the whole room of himself out of the walls at once and still not enough.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The biggest one since '96. Too many of them. Not enough of me. The old math.

**PAGE 26 — the fourth cut. The grip.**
A fist finds his hair at the crown and *hauls* — they don't grab your head unless they mean to open it. The razor goes in at the crown and unzips to the nape. One long cold seam.
> SFX: *RRRIP*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): They don't grab your head unless they mean to bare what's under it.

**PAGE 27 — THE FLIP. Full-bleed. The world inverts.**
Sky where the asphalt should be. The grey street hanging over him like a ceiling of water. **(RED)** sheeting *up* now instead of down, up over his brow, into one eye. His head wrenched back, throat pulled long, neck bared to a sky that's become a floor. **The page is now upside down; the reader turns the book.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Everything I've ever known about which way is down files for divorce all at once.

**PAGES 28–29 — the deepest dive. (Veil render, inverted.)**
Upside down is not a camera trick — it's the true shape of the thing, where grief lives, where the boy has been this whole time. A good structure hanging upside down from its own foundation, every window black now, not one left to argue through. And in the center of the inverted dark, a small figure — Keystone, silhouette, never face — walked patiently to the lip of his own highest tower, told in that warm chosen voice that *the drop is a door. The fall is a way out. Stepping off is the same as being held.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The worst thing a father will ever know is being able to feel the exact inch his son is standing on and not being able to touch it.

**PAGE 30 — the room comes apart with the world.**
Arc's gate falls off into the black. Weld's hands open on nothing, on nothing, on nothing. Every piece he called a crew — furniture in a room with one man in it, falling past him toward a sky that used to be a floor.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He's alone. He was always alone. A man in an inverted room, reaching up-that-is-down for a boy he can hear breathing and cannot find.

**PAGE 31 — THE BOTTOM. The lowest the book goes.**
Head back, throat long, world flipped, crew scattered, the boy one inch from the door that isn't a door. The blade at the crown starting its travel. The drop under the boy's foot opening like a mouth. **The only color is the blood running up.** Bottom of the page (which is now the top): something is coming over the roof of a car.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Something is always, in this family, coming over the top of the car.
> **But not yet. FADE on the bared neck.**

## CUT 5 — THE NECK / THE SHIELD
*The kill shot that doesn't land · over the car · the turn: victim → protector.*
**→ SCRIPT NOTE: THE BOOK RIGHTS ITSELF HERE. As the man turns, the page turns back right-side-up. This section introduces WHITE as grace.**

**PAGE 32 — pick up on the bared neck. Still inverted. The razor traveling.**
Both necks — his in the grey, the boy's in the inverted dark. Two finishes on one held breath. The drop opening like a mouth. And something comes over the top of the car.

**PAGE 33 — '96, THE HAND THAT STOPS THE BLADE. Splash.**
Blown-white '96. **Tapioca** — an ear already half gone and pouring **(RED)**, the back of him already opened — comes clean over the *roof of a car,* like something torn out of a comic book dropped onto a real highway, and lands on the man with the razor. **The last cut does not fall.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I was fifteen. I learned the only theology I've ever fully trusted. *The finish doesn't have to land. Somebody can get a hand in. Somebody can come over the car.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I forgot it, the way you forget the thing that saved you. I've been falling all five cuts *because* I forgot it. I remember it now.

**PAGE 34 — THE TURN. The world rights. (The book flips back.)**
He stops being the neck. Something that spent thirty years on the ground turns over, gets a hand up into his own inverted sky — and the world **groans and rights itself.** Down goes back to down. Panels rotate upright as the reader turns the book back.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A man who decides to be the shield instead of the wound just changed which way gravity works in his own book.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): I was the boy who got saved by someone coming over a car. Now I'm the one coming over the car.

**PAGE 35 — THE SHIELD. Splash. First real WHITE.**
Down in the veiled dark, his hand finally lands — not on the phantom (you can't punch a thing made of warmth), on the **inch,** between the boy and the door-that-isn't-a-door. He lifts a **shield,** not a fist. He carried every black frame of the pit up into a **(WHITE)** light where a judge can read it — the phantom's own patient blueprint made the case against it. A wall of prayer lifted over a child they were never even told the name of. And holding the shield up when his own cut arm gives out: the one Light that has never lied about what's on the far side of a door.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Tried to take my son. I lifted my shield.**

**PAGE 36 — the crew reassembles as one. Splash — every fragment turning the same way.**
Arc's blown gate becomes a *wall.* Weld's empty hands find the one job left to a hand that can't fix the past: *guard the future.* He thought he ran a crew; he was alone in a room with himself — and he stands up out of that room not less alone, but **differently alone: one man, every piece of him faced the same direction, all of it a shield.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Not less alone. Differently alone. All of it a shield.

**PAGE 37 — not over, but turned.**
A knife still coming — one more scar, the deepest, the one that stays. The phantom's hand still at the edge; a thing that patient doesn't leave because a shield went up. But now the blood means two things: the blood the enemy spills — and the blood a father, a friend over a car, a Light on a hill will pour out *on purpose* to get between the dark and a child. One floods the frame. The other holds the door.
> **Hold on the shield, up, the boy behind it. FADE — not to black. To (WHITE).**

## CUT 6 — THE KNIFE THAT STAYS
*The last cut · stabbed · bleeding out · the floor drops all the way · the boy that made the man.*

**PAGE 38 — the phantom's last patient cut.**
It waits for the arm holding the shield to be the arm already cut in four places. It waits for the exact tired inch. And it doesn't slash. It **stabs.** In the back. Low. And where every other blade drew its line and pulled away — this one goes to the handle and **stays.**
> SFX: *(no sound. just cold turning warm.)*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A knife with no intention of leaving.

**PAGE 39 — he recognizes it. The reveal that turns a death into a birth.**
His hand goes back to the handle and the handle is *familiar* — worn to the shape of a grip he's felt his whole life without once looking at it.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **This knife has been in my back since 1996. It never came out.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Every cut in this whole book was the body remembering *this.* The one that stayed. The true spine of the book. The crease the whole thing folds along.

**PAGE 40 — the key turns. THE FLOOR DROPS ALL THE WAY.**
The other cuts flashed him a half-second and snapped him back. Not this one. It's a key, it's been in the lock the whole time, and now it *turns.* Full-bleed: the grey gives out completely, no snapping back, no half-second — he falls the whole distance, past the boy's veiled dark, past every floor, all the way down to a bright day and six lanes and a window already down.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): And it does not let me leave until it's finished.

**PAGE 41 — the thesis, paid in full.**
A boy took a blade that should have ended him and *did not end.* Drove himself home leaking, and walked into a room full of light with the wound still open, and voted to keep living. He wasn't surviving by accident — he was being *forged* for a night thirty years on, when a phantom would try to take his son and a shield would have to go up, and the only man who could hold it with four fresh cuts and a knife in his back was one who'd already learned, at fifteen, on a highway, that **you can bleed out everything you've got and still come over the car for someone else.**
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The knife that stays didn't cripple me. It *credentialed* me. I could be the shield in cut five *because* of the knife in cut six.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The boy made the man. The wound made the wall.

**PAGE 42 — now, one last time. Risen.**
He comes up out of '96 for the last time — not snapped back, *risen,* the way you come up out of deep water when you've finally decided to. Still on one knee. Still bleeding. Knife still where it's always been and where it's going to stay. Shield still up. The boy still behind it. And the phantom's hand at the edge of the frame, for the first time in six cuts, **withdrawing** — not because it lost (a thing that patient never fully loses), but because it met the one thing it can't account for: a wound that made a man *stronger* than the taking of him.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): **Tried to take my son. I lifted my shield. And the shield was made out of everything that ever tried to take me.**

**PAGE 43 — the closing image of the wound map.**
The blood is still the only color in the world — but read down the spine of the book from the binding out, look what it spells now: not a man being destroyed. A man being **built.**
> **Hold on the knife that stays, the shield that stays, the boy behind it. FADE TO (WHITE). HOLD THE WHITE.**

## THE FLOOR — '96, THE ORIGIN
*Cut 6 drops all the way here. Told straight through, unbroken, first cut to last. Rendered in blown-white daylight; blood the only color.*

**PAGE 44 — six lanes, 4:15.**
Blown-white. Warm day, windows down, the kind of afternoon with no idea what it's about to become. The father's voice in the truck: *Take this to the room. Don't touch it till you're there.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): An errand. That's all it was supposed to be.

**PAGE 45 — the glance, the bird, the rage.**
Two cars breathing side by side at speed. A woman driving. A man folded in the passenger seat. A look, less than a look — taken wrong. The bird comes back over the door, and behind it the rage, out of all proportion to a glance on a Tuesday.

**PAGE 46 — the wave.**
The tell nobody clocks in time: the wave isn't *for* them. It goes back. A second carload, called up from behind like dogs off a chain. **Never one man's temper — a hunt with more teeth than the road could see.**

**PAGE 47 — CUT 1, the arm. Splash.**
The long car whips in front and stands on the brakes. Boxed. Before the boy's brain files the shape as a *blade,* it's through the open window and through the arm — muscle, then the deep cold wrongness of bone — and the **(RED)** comes up all at once, sheeting off the elbow onto the door, into the white. First blood on the highway.
> SFX: *SHNK* / *HOOOONK*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): The horns will not stop for any of it.

**PAGE 48 — "GET OUT — WE'RE SURROUNDED."**
Tapioca's voice. Two words that end the afternoon. No version where you stay in the box — so they go *out,* into the middle of six screaming lanes with the whole carload closing.

**PAGE 49 — the first two.**
One jumps him. One swings — a phone still in the fist, plastic cracking off his head, a white flash, the taste of pennies. He gets his hands in anyway, picks the man clean up, slams him down, drops a knee, closes a hand on the throat and squeezes — heels drumming asphalt — and looks up.

**PAGE 50 — they've got Tapioca.**
He lets go of the sure thing to save his friend — the only math he's ever known — and the second he turns loose he takes one from the blind side. Gets under the new one, lifts him, and **throws him into the oncoming lane.** A body in the air, then brakes, then horns swallowing whatever sound the road makes.

**PAGE 51 — cuts 2 & 3, the spine and the shoulder blade.**
Someone yanks his shirt up from behind. The warm scratch that isn't a scratch — a line down the spine, another across the shoulder blade — so soft-feeling and so wrong, the blood running warm into his waistband before the pain files its report. **The box-cutter line. The binding.**

**PAGE 52 — cut 4, the scalp.**
A fist finds his hair and hauls. The blade opens the back of his head, crown to nape, one long unzipping — the world tips, sky where asphalt should be, a warm red curtain over one eye. His head wrenched back, throat pulled long, bared to the light. **This is where the night was always driving.**

**PAGE 53 — cut 5, the shield. Splash.**
Tapioca over the roof of a car — over it, one ear already half-gone and pouring, the back of him already opened — clears the whole car and lands on the man with the blade, into his head, and **the last cut never falls.** The throat closes. The boy comes back down into his own body inside a lake of **(RED)**.

**PAGE 54 — all red.**
Not choreography anymore — a drowning. Swinging every direction at once, blind in one eye, can't tell his blood from theirs from the road's — until one more car locks its brakes and *screams,* long and human, and something in that sound breaks the spell, and the seven scatter back into the traffic they came out of.

**PAGE 55 — the cost.**
Tapioca: an ear cut clean off, a knife left in his back, cut around the waist and the leg. Standing. Him: the arm to the bone, the spine, the shoulder blade, the scalp crown to nape, a knife in his own back. Standing. **Two of them upright in the middle of a stopped highway, counting their own leaks. Three of the seven down in the road they thought was theirs.**

**PAGE 56 — the drive.**
The part nobody would believe if it weren't true. Into the vehicle. Manual gearbox. Every shift a fresh scream from an arm more open than closed — hand to the knob, muscle and bone arguing through every gear — and he drives. **(RED)** filling the seat, on the wheel. To the next lot. Out. The ground comes up to meet him. A crowd gathers, too late and too loud.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *You're losing—*
> **FADE TO (RED). THEN TO BLACK.**
