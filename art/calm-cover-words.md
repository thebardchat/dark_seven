# DARK SEVEN: DARK CORRIDORS — THE CALM COVER (the mask)
*The word set for the front cover — the lie the first page detonates. Everything here is calm, controlled, respectable, inviting. It should read as perfectly reassuring on the surface, and reveal itself as a lie the instant you turn the page to the bloody first page. Bone/white-dominant palette. The Devil does not win.*

## DESIGN NOTES
- **Palette inverts:** cover is bone/white-dominant (grace, the lie, the pristine surface); first page is black/blood-dominant (the truth).
- **Figure:** the CALM twin — same man, same white suit, composed and clean, no blood. (Generate likeness-locked in Gemini; then cut out + build to match the bloody first page layout.)
- **The whole trick:** the reader holds a clean, calm, reassuring cover — turns one page — and it rips open into blood. The mask and the face are the same man. That page-turn IS the book.
