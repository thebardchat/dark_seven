# DARK SEVEN: DARK CORRIDORS — CHARACTER DESIGN SHEETS
### Artist reference bible · lock these, draw to them every page
*Design law overrides everything below: the world is BLACK & WHITE; the ONLY color that ever appears is BLOOD RED; WHITE is reserved for grace (the shield, the Light, the locked-safe door, the big-house window). No other color, ever. '96 renders in blinding blown-out daylight white. Blood is the one exception in every scene.*

---

## GLOBAL RULES (read before drawing anyone)
- **ART STYLE — LOCKED: Style C, "GTA cel."** Bold clean black outlines, flat controlled grey fills, vivid defined blood-red. This is the INTERIOR house style — draw every page in it (chosen because it prints best: bold linework + flat tones hold at page size on any paper and keep the likeness). **Style A ("Ink Noir" — heavy black spatter, photographic greys) is COVERS / full-page splashes only, premium stock.** Do not render interior pages in the soft/rotoscope pink-blood look (it washes out in print). Print target: 600 DPI line art, red as a defined spot/solid, rich blacks.
- **Three inks, no fourth.** Black = the world/the dark. Red = blood only (never a red object). White = grace, rare and earned. (Full paste-ready rule: `STRICT-COLOR-PROMPT.md`.)
- **The likeness lock — THE MAN IS SHANE.** The detective is rendered as the author's own face. Work from his reference photos/art (see below). Do NOT drift the face toward a generic actor. This is the single most important continuity rule in the book.
- **The veil.** Two characters are NEVER shown fully: **Keystone** (the boy) and **the Fractured Architect** (the phantom). Draw *around* them, never *at* them. Breaking the veil breaks the book (and the real-world protection behind it).
- **Crew = mind.** Arc and Weld are not separate people — they are facets of the man's own head, drawn as shadow-selves that share his silhouette.
- **Strong blacks, bold shapes (per Style C).** High contrast with figures emerging from black — but achieved through **bold clean linework and flat controlled tones**, not painterly ink-wash or fine spatter (that's Style A, covers only). When in doubt, more black — in clean shapes.

## 1 · THE MAN  *(the detective — the anchor; nameless)*
**Likeness:** Shane himself. LOCKED. Broad, heavy, powerful build — not lean. Weathered, lived-in face. Thick grey-and-dark beard, full. Grey-streaked hair swept back. Hard, tired eyes that have decided something. A man who has been hit a lot and is still standing.
**Two wardrobes:**
- **NOW (interior/rain):** a **grey** coat, rain-soaked, worn — the working detective. This is the day-to-day of the wound map.
- **Iconic / cover / hero shots:** the **white suit** over a black shirt — deliberate *Scarface / Tony Montana* frame (there is an in-story reason). Immaculate on the calm cover; blood-spattered on the first page.
**Signature:** he holds the **right arm** a little away from his body — the old '96 arm wound never sat right. His weapon is **FISTS OF RAGE** — bare, oversized, scarred knuckles, no gun, ever. In rage he is drenched in blood; the fists drip.
**The scars (his true model sheet — the wound map lives on his body):** arm (through the forearm), a long **box-cutter line down the spine** (this IS the book's binding), across the shoulder blade, **scalp crown-to-nape**, the neck, and low in the back a **buried knife that never came out**. Over all of it, on his back: a **Celtic cross tattoo** covering the spine scar — grace laid over the wound. (This is real; it is the thesis in his skin.)
**Expression range:** still/controlled (the mask) → enraged snarl (fists of rage) → on-one-knee, bleeding, shield up (the end).
**Color:** monochrome; the only red on him is his own blood.
**Reference art:** `the-man-v2.webp`, `the-man-poster-v3.webp`, `the-man-bloodyfists-edit.webp`, `shane noir portrait.png` (the face — also the cover), `cover_plain.png`, `the-man-cutout.webp` / `the-man-calm-cutout.webp`.

## 2 · THE OLD SOLDIER  *(the First & Last Witness — the grandfather)*
**Role:** disabled veteran, lives in the apartment on the side of the house; watches the movie first (opening frame) and lets it go last (closing frame).
**Build/face:** older, dignified, carries an unspoken weight in how he holds himself. Not frail — a soldier who has *seen* wounds, in the field, in person. Rendered mostly in grey, often swallowed by the dark of his room.
**Wardrobe:** plain, at-home — the recliner-and-window man. No costume.
**Signature setting:** the recliner that knows his shape; the one window with the warm **(WHITE)** big-house light beyond it.
**Color:** the ONLY red that touches him is the movie's blood thrown on his face by the screen — red, then grey, then red. By the closing frame the red is spent and only the white of home remains.
**Veil note:** honor his service as archetype; invent no specific war/medal.
**Reference:** the man's face aged/weathered; no dedicated art yet — brief an artist from PAGE 1–5 of the First Witness pencil.

## 3 · TAPIOCA  *(the '96 friend who came over the car)*
**Role:** the boy who, already gutted, came over the roof of a car to stop the killing blow — the origin of the book's only theology, "the finish doesn't have to land." Named on purpose (naming is rescue).
**'96 look — Shane's exact account:** Shane's real best friend. Torn open in FIVE places and *still fought*: **ear cut clean off**, a **knife in the back low by the lumbar**, **cut front-to-back around the waist**, **around the thigh**, and the **tricep** — and he **flew over the FRONT of a car and struck the killer in the FOREHEAD** to stop the blade at Shane's bared neck. Draw the save: mid-flight over a car hood, arms out, ruined and pouring blood, impossibly airborne — *the most torn-apart man in the fight spending what he had left for his friend.* His likeness in the reference art is close to the man's — that's Shane's call; render as Tapioca, his own person.
**⭐⭐ MASTER LIKENESS REFERENCE (Tapioca's REAL face, 2026-07-12): `tapioca-real-face-ref.jpg`** — this is a real photo of Shane's actual best friend Tapioca. ALWAYS pass this as the reference image when generating Tapioca so every render matches his true likeness. His real features: lean/fit build (NOT heavy), short dark hair pulled back, short beard/goatee, defined jaw and cheekbones. Companion noir portrait (matches the man's cover portrait, dramatic Rembrandt lighting, white jacket/black shirt): `tapioca-noir-portrait.png` — the two men are a matched pair, the two brothers at the book's heart.
**How to lock his face every time:** `python gemini-gen.py "pose/scene prompt, keep the EXACT face from the reference" out.png tapioca-real-face-ref.jpg`
**Deprecated:** earlier made-up comic faces (`tapioca-own-face-*.png`, `tapioca-styleC-hero.png`) — they are NOT his real face; use the real-face ref instead.
**Also:** `tapioca-96-leap-*.png` — the iconic '96 save (over the car hood, torn open, mid-flight).
**Present-day (if shown):** a grown man who carries the wound inside; per the locked TAPIOCA sheet, his interior story is **his** to tell — the book leaves him a **blank white page.** Do not over-render his present self; reserve, respect.
**Color:** monochrome; blood only.
**Reference:** see `tapioca.md` (his address + the blank-page device). Visual is new — brief from the '96 Floor pencil (the save, the cost).

## 4 · ARC  *(the crew = the mind — the Gate / the Wall)*
**What he is:** a facet of the man's own head, drawn as a **shadow-self that shares the man's silhouette** — same shoulders, same build, half-lit, a reflection stepping out of him. NOT a separate person.
**Role/stance:** the warden at the threshold — always drawn at an edge, a doorway, a gate. He decides what gets in. When he fails, the gate blows off its hinges; when the man becomes the shield, Arc's gate **becomes a wall** (Cut 5).
**Signature:** a gatekeeper's posture; arc-light eyes / data-cable motif can carry over subtly from his MEGA-bot origin, but keep him HUMAN-shadow, not a cartoon robot in this book.
**Color:** monochrome shadow.

## 5 · WELD  *(the crew = the mind — the Hands / the Fixer)*
**What he is:** another shadow-self of the man, **defined by the hands** — a maker's hands, always reaching to fix. Same silhouette/face as the man, half-lit.
**Role:** he repairs what's broken — until Cut 2, where his hands reach into the boy's dark and find **no seam to weld, nothing to grip.** The hand that never failed, opening and closing on smoke. In Cut 5 his empty hands find the one job left: **guard the future.**
**Signature:** the hands. Draw the hands doing/failing/guarding.
**Color:** monochrome shadow.

## 6 · THE FRACTURED ARCHITECT  *(the phantom — the predator)* — **VEILED, NEVER A FACE**
**Absolute rule:** never fully in frame. It is only ever: **a hand** (ringless, open, patient — it *offers*, it does not grab) at the very edge of the panel, and **a warm light** thrown from off-panel. Never a body. Never a face. Never named.
**Its "presence":** the false-moon bulb where the real moon should hang; windows going black one at a time under a patient brush; borrowed grey chemical weather smothering a true sky. The reader feels it in the chest and is handed zero facts. That is the horror AND the protection.
**Caption voice:** THE DARK — a torn caption box with red dripping from one corner; quiet, almost kind, counts the cuts down.
**Color:** black and the warm off-panel glow (rendered in white/grey, never a color). No red of its own — the red it causes belongs to its victims.

## 7 · KEYSTONE  *(the boy)* — **VEILED, NEVER FACE-FRONT**
**Absolute rule:** never a face, never named on the page. Draw him as **a small silhouette, back to us, dwarfed by vast architecture** — a good, honest **cathedral** with all its doors unlocked (because it never learned it needed locks). We see his *world* (the inverted cathedral, the blacked windows, the lip of the highest tower), never the act, never his face.
**Meaning of the name:** the crown-stone of the arch — the piece the Architect tried to pull; **it held.** Load-bearing, not a victim. Draw him as the stone that carries weight, even small and turned away.
**Color:** monochrome; if any red touches his world it is minimal and symbolic, never explicit.

## 8 · THE SEVEN  *(the '96 attackers)* — faceless
The carload from the highway. Draw them **faceless / near-featureless** — shapes out of the traffic, hands with blades. They are a hunt, not characters. Three of them end the night "down in the road they thought was theirs."

## MODEL-SHEET CHECKLIST (what to draw for each real character)
For **the man**, **the old soldier**, and **Tapioca** an artist should produce:
1. **Turnaround** (front / 3-4 / profile / back — the man's back is critical: the scar map + Celtic cross).
2. **Expression sheet** (for the man: still/mask → snarl/rage → bleeding resolve).
3. **Hands** (for the man's fists of rage; for Weld's maker's hands).
4. **Wardrobe states** (the man: grey rain coat / clean white suit / blood-spattered white suit).
5. **Scar map** (the man, dedicated — every cut placed correctly; the spine line = the binding).
Arc & Weld are drawn from the man's turnaround as shadow-variants. The Architect and Keystone get **no turnaround** — only their veiled vocabulary (hand+light; silhouette+cathedral).
