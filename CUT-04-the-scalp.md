# CUT 4 — THE SCALP
### NOW (the huge fight) ⇄ the deepest dive · crown to nape · the world goes upside down
*Wound map, cut 4. The deepest fall into the boy yet — and the only cut where the present-day war rages full-blast WHILE we fall. Hair grabbed, scalp opened crown to nape, neck bared for the finish. This is the upside-down floor. Black, white, and blood. Veil is law.*

---

**NOW. NO DESK ANYMORE. ALL WAR.**

The file did what files like this do — it stopped being paper and became a fight. However the man went looking for the thing at the edge of the frame, the thing came back with company, and now there is no office, no rain on a window, only bodies in the grey and the man in the middle of them throwing the arm that still works.

This is the biggest one since '96. He knows it the way you know weather. Too many of them. Not enough of him. The old math. **The whole crew is up** — Arc screaming at a gate that's already off its hinges, Weld swinging a hand at everything and connecting with half of it, the whole room of himself out of the walls at once and still not enough, because they were only ever pieces of one tired man and the dark brought a *crowd.*

**THE FOURTH CUT**

A fist finds his hair.

Not a blade first — a *grip,* the whole hand closing in the hair at the crown and *hauling,* and that's the one that means the end is here, because they don't grab your head unless they mean to open it and bare what's under it. The razor goes in at the crown and unzips to the nape, crown to neck, one long cold seam across the back of the skull —

and the world flips.

**UPSIDE DOWN**

Sky where the asphalt should be. The grey street hanging over him like a ceiling of water. Blood — the only color left in creation — sheeting up now instead of down, up over his brow, up into one eye, and everything he's ever known about which way is down files for divorce all at once. His head wrenched back on the fist. His throat pulled long. His neck bared to a sky that's become a floor. This is the posture the whole night was always driving toward. Head back. Neck open. The finish loaded.

And that is the exact instant the book drops him all the way to the bottom.

**THE DEEPEST DIVE**

Because upside down is not a camera trick in this book. Upside down is the true shape of the thing. It's where grief actually lives. It's where the boy has been this whole time, and now — head inverted, neck bared, one breath from the finish — the man finally falls all the way down to him and sees, from the inside, veiled and total and nameless, the floor of it:

*A world turned all the way over.* A good structure hanging upside down from its own foundation, every window black now, not one left to argue through. The false light swallowed whole. The borrowed weather total. And in the center of the inverted dark, a boy walked patiently, lovingly, to the lip of his own highest tower and told — in that warm voice, that chosen voice, that voice that never once felt like a blade — that the drop is a door. That the fall is a way out. That down is up and the ground is the sky and stepping off is the same as being held.

The man falls through that with his own neck bared thirty years and an ocean away, and he cannot right it, cannot flip the world back over, cannot get one hand between his boy and the edge — because down here everything is felt and nothing can be reached, and the worst thing a father will ever know is being able to *feel* the exact inch his son is standing on and not being able to touch it.

**THE ROOM WITH HIMSELF, TIPPING**

And the crew goes with the world. Upside down, they come apart. Arc's gate falls off into the black. Weld's hands open on nothing, on nothing, on nothing. Every informant, every piece of muscle, every voice he told himself was a crew — they were only ever furniture in a room with one man in it, and the room is upside down now, and the furniture is falling past him toward a sky that used to be a floor. *He's alone. He was always alone. A man in an inverted room reaching up-that-is-down for a boy he can hear breathing and cannot find.*

Both necks bared. His in the grey. The boy's in the inverted dark. Two finishes loaded, thirty years apart, on the same held breath.

**THE BOTTOM**

This is the lowest the book goes. Head back, throat long, world flipped, crew scattered, the boy one inch from the door that isn't a door — and the blade at the crown starting its travel toward the throat, and the drop under the boy's foot opening like a mouth —

**HOLD. UPSIDE DOWN. THE ONLY COLOR IS THE BLOOD RUNNING UP.**

Something is coming over the top of the car.

Something is always, in this family, coming over the top of the car.

**BUT NOT YET. FADE ON THE BARED NECK.**

### Note on placement
Cut 4 of the wound map — the bottom. Per the skeleton, this is the only cut where the present-day war rages at full volume *while* we fall, so the man is mid-massacre (scalp opened crown to nape, neck bared) at the exact moment he reaches the deepest floor of the boy. The upside-down motif is spent here, full and literal, on both layers at once: his world inverts under the scalp wound; the boy's world is *already* inverted, and that inversion is revealed as the true shape of the dark — the false light total, every window black, the boy walked to the edge of the steeple and told the drop is a door (veiled completely — the decompensation rendered as pure inverted-world feeling, no fact, no name). The crew-as-mind pays off hard: upside down, the crew comes apart and the "room with himself" is exposed — he was always alone. Deliberate cliffhanger: both necks bared, the save (cut 5, the shield) glimpsed coming over the car and withheld. Escalation locked — this is the deepest and darkest cut; the only direction left is the turn.
