What this is

Volume One watched the world. Volume Two listened to it. Volume Three bleeds.

A dark noir blood comic. Black and white, and the only color is blood. Six cuts on a nameless detective, each deeper than the last, each one a door into an older wound — all of them hung off the one scar down his spine, which is the binding of the book itself.

Two things happen at once, neither named on the surface: a case that's really about the thing that came for his son, and a highway thirty years gone that never stopped bleeding.

The Devil does not win.

The design law

Three inks, no fourth.

Black

The world, and the dark. The default state of everything.

White

Grace — the shield, the Light, rescue. Reserved and rare. Earn it.

Red

Blood, and blood only. If it's red, it's bleeding. Nothing else is ever red.

The one sanctioned extension: the red neon Celtic cross — grace and blood at once, burning in the dark. It is red because it is bought with blood.

Who's in it

No names, still.

The Man

The nameless detective. Takes the six cuts. Was the boy on the highway. Becomes the shield.

Tapioca

The friend who came over the roof of the car in '96 — the origin of the only theology the book trusts: the finish doesn't have to land.

Arc & Weld

The detective's "crew," who are really fragments of his own mind. Arc keeps the gate; Weld is the hands. He thinks he runs a crew — he's alone in a room with himself, until every piece turns the same way and becomes a shield.

Keystone

The boy. Veiled — never named, never face-front, his ordeal never shown, only felt. The crown-stone the Architect tried to pull. It held.

The Fractured Architect

The thing that came for Keystone. A hand and a warm light. Never a face. Never a body.

The Witness

The old soldier in the recliner who watches it first and lets it go last. The frame around the fall.
