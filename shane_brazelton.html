The Author

Concrete.
By Day.

Shane Brazelton dispatches 14 concrete trucks across North Alabama before most people are awake. He records his books on the pre-dawn commute — stream-of-consciousness, voice-to-text, doing 70 on the highway in the dark.

"Nobody asked him to build this.
He did it anyway."

The manuscripts get pressed into noir by an AI built on a Raspberry Pi in a closet in Hazel Green, Alabama. No publishing deal. No literary agent. No permission.

The work is dark but not hopeless. Cynical but anchored. The Devil does not win in these pages. That's not a spoiler — that's the whole constitution.
