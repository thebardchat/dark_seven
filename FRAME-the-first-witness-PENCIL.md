# DARK SEVEN — THE FIRST WITNESS
## Pencil-Ready Script · the opening bookend · the book's first pages (pp. 1–5)
*Opens the loop that THE LAST WITNESS closes. The old soldier — grandfather, disabled veteran, the apartment on the side of the house — watches it first. This is the calm before the wound map; give it weight and air, then let the first red land like a struck match.*

> **RENDER — THE OPEN.** Black, white, and grey — the world drained. The book's FIRST red does not appear until PANEL where the movie's first cut hits (PAGE 2). Before that: no color at all, the pristine hush. The one grace-note is **(WHITE)** — the warm big-house light in the window (home, the far side of the door); this same white is all that's left at the very end in THE LAST WITNESS. Structural rule to honor: the book OPENS with the first red thrown onto this old face and CLOSES with the last red gone and only the white of home.
> **THE ERRAND MATCH-CUT.** His hand on the recliner armrest = the same hand that, thirty years back, closed on a truck door in '96. Render the '96 flash in blown-out daylight WHITE, one panel, then back to grey. (This is the same hand THE LAST WITNESS shows at rest, done gripping.)
> **TWO VOICES ONLY.** THE MAN (clean black caption box, white type — the book's narrator voice). THE DARK (torn box, red dripping from the bottom corner — quiet, never shouting). No omniscient narrator.
> **THE VEIL holds even here.** When the movie's cuts show on the screen-within-the-panel, we see them only as light and red thrown on the OLD MAN'S FACE — never the screen content directly. We watch him watch. The horror is on his face, not on the screen.

## PAGE 1 — THE APARTMENT ON THE SIDE OF THE HOUSE
*Full-bleed splash. Establish. No red anywhere. The hush before.*

**SPLASH.** Black on black. One room — a recliner that knows the exact shape of the man in it, rendered in grey, most of him swallowed by the dark. He holds himself the way men hold a weight they never set down out loud. Behind him, one window — and through it, small and far and warm, the **(WHITE)** glow of the big house: a kitchen light, the boys' windows. Near the ones he'd die for. Not underfoot. The only light in his little country comes from a house he is guarding by watching.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): There's a door on the side of the house that goes to its own small country.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Near the ones he'd die for. Not underfoot. He spent a life learning the difference between guarding a thing and crowding it.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): If one man on earth is qualified to be first to watch a story told entirely in scars — it's the one in the recliner.

---

## PAGE 2 — THE SCREEN COMES ON
*3 tall panels. The first red is a struck match in a colorless room.*

**PANEL 1.** The screen ignites off-panel; its cold light hits the wall and the side of the recliner. Black-and-white pours across the room. The old face turns toward it — lit grey, unreadable, patient.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He has seen what a wound is. Not on a screen. In the field. On boys younger than the one who made this movie.

**PANEL 2.** TIGHT on the old face, grey on grey, the screen-glow flickering in his eyes. He does not brace. He has already decided to watch all of it.
> SFX (soft, bleeding from the screen): *—the horns, far off—*

**PANEL 3.** The movie's first cut lands — and the first **(RED)** in the whole book is thrown across his face. Red on the old man. The single hot note in a drained world. Then it lets him go grey again. Do NOT show the screen; show the red on HIM.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(torn box, one red drip)* That's your boy's blood. Look at it. You're going to look at all of it.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Red. Grey. Red. Grey. An old soldier lit, off and on, by the bleeding of a boy he sent out a door thirty years ago.

---

## PAGE 3 — THE ERRAND, FROM THE OTHER SIDE
*4 panels, intercut NOW ⇄ '96. The unbearable center: he was the one who sent the boy.*

**PANEL 1.** His hand tightens on the recliner armrest — knuckles, age, grip.

**PANEL 2 — MATCH CUT, blown-out WHITE '96.** The same hand, younger, closing on a truck door handle in blinding afternoon daylight. One panel of white. A boy in the driver's seat, unbothered, alive.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *Take this to the room. Don't touch it till you're there.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): An errand. That's all it was supposed to be.

**PANEL 3 — back to the grey room.** The old face, wet-eyed maybe, or just still. The screen-red touches him again and passes.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(dripping)* You handed him a chore on a bright day. You didn't know which afternoons come back different.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): No father does.

**PANEL 4.** TIGHT — his hand, still on the armrest, not moving. He would give anything to have kept the boy home that day. He couldn't have known. He'd give anything anyway.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He's carried a lot of weights for a lot of years. That one he's carried quietest of all.

---

## PAGE 4 — THREE MEN, ONE DOOR
*Wide. The whole line, in one image. The generational spine.*

**PANEL 1 — WIDE, three figures / one doorway repeated across the page.** A grandfather in a door (grey, the recliner's silhouette). A son in a door (the man — coat, the set of the shoulders we'll come to know). A boy — **Keystone, silhouette only, back to us, never a face** — in the third door, stepping into hard clean **(WHITE)** light. One red thread runs from frame to frame, linking all three: wound and strength, the same color coming down the line.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): Three men. One door, over and over.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): A grandfather who sent a son. A son who couldn't shield his own. A boy who's in the light now because the shield finally went up.

**PANEL 2 — tight on the old man watching.** He understands the whole line now, sitting in the dark, first, alone.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): You cannot control the road. You can only teach a child to walk into the room anyway — and be there, near, not crowding, when they come back different.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): It came down the line like a wound and like a strength. In this family they were always the same red.

---

## PAGE 5 — HE DOESN'T LOOK AWAY
*3 panels + hold. The oath of the first witness. Sets the fade the LAST WITNESS will pay off.*

**PANEL 1.** On the screen (still only shown as it lands on his face): the worst of it comes — the scalp, the knife that stays — huge **(RED)** thrown across him. Another man would look away. He doesn't. He's seen worse and loved less. This one he loves with his whole cracked heart.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He does the only thing a first witness can do for a story like this.

**PANEL 2.** Tight, unflinching. Red then grey then red on the old face. He receives it. He lets it be true and doesn't flinch it smaller.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): He believes it. He receives it.
> CAPTION (THE DARK): *(smaller now, guttering)* …

**PANEL 3.** The screen-red on his face, and behind him — steady, unchanged through all of it — the **(WHITE)** big-house light in the window. The blood on his face; the home at his back.
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *I saw it. I saw what happened to you. I saw what you did about it.*
> CAPTION (THE MAN): *I believe every cut.*

**HOLD — the old face, (RED) then grey. The (WHITE) big-house light in the window behind him. FADE.**
> *We leave him here, watching, believing, the light of home steady at his back. The wound map begins. THE LAST WITNESS returns to this exact chair — and by then the red is spent, and only the white of home is left.*

### Note on placement
Opening bookend, pp. 1–5. Now penciled to full density (was the last section at "blocked"). Mirror-built with THE LAST WITNESS: OPEN = first red thrown on his face, close-frame ends on the window light kept steady behind the blood; CLOSE = last red spent, only the white of home. The '96 errand match-cut plants the grandfather AS the man who unknowingly sent the boy, so the first witness carries the origin guilt before the reader ever sees the highway. Veil intact — the movie's violence is shown only as light/red on the watcher's face; we watch him watch. Grace-white reserved to the big-house window. The Devil does not win.
