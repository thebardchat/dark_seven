# CUT 1 — THE ARM
### NOW · the frame · first blood · teaches the reader to brace
*Wound map, cut 1. Opens: the case surfaces / the boy enters / the devil's hand at the edge of frame. Black, white, and blood — nothing else gets color. The veil is up: real specifics never touch the page.*

---

**FADE IN — NOW. RAIN. NO COLOR IN IT.**

The city comes in black and white through a window that hasn't been clean in years. Streetlight, wet asphalt, the grey of everything. The man sits in it the way you sit in a thing you've stopped noticing. Older now. Same body — you can tell by how he holds the right arm, a little away from himself, like it still owes him something.

He runs a crew. That's what he tells himself. Informants in the dark, a hand that does the work when the work needs doing. **Arc** keeps the gate — nothing reaches the man that Arc hasn't sniffed first, turned over, cleared or killed. That's the arrangement. That's always been the arrangement.

So when the case slides under the door, Arc's supposed to stop it.

Arc doesn't stop it.

**THE CASE**

No color to it either. A plain thing. A file with no name on it that the man knows the weight of before he touches it, the way you know your own name called across a parking lot. He doesn't open it. He doesn't have to. Some cases you don't investigate. Some cases you *recognize.*

*Nothing to do with him.* That's what the surface says. That's what the veil is for.

Everything to do with him.

**THE HAND AT THE EDGE**

And there — at the very edge of the frame, where the streetlight gives out — a hand. Just a hand. Patient. Ringless. Resting on nothing, the way the devil rests when it's already sure it has somewhere to be and all night to get there. It doesn't reach. It doesn't have to reach. It grew its own little light and hung it out front, shaped like whatever the dark needed that day, and it's just *waiting* — exactly where something was already going to swim.

The man sees it. Pretends the window's just wet.

**Arc, from the gate, quiet:** *I can't hold this one. It's already inside. It came in wearing your own face.*

**THE AMBUSH SNAPS SHUT**

He should've heard it. Thirty years running crews and he doesn't hear the room change until the room is already closed.

A shape out of the grey. Then the shape has a hand, and the hand has a line of light in it, and before the man's brain can file the shape of it as a *blade* —

— it's through his sleeve and through the arm.

**FIRST BLOOD.**

And now the only color in the whole world is the color that comes up out of him. Red on a grey floor. Red on a grey coat. The single hot chromatic wrong note in a black-and-white room, blooming down the forearm, off the elbow, one bright drop and then the string of them.

**THE SCAR UNDERNEATH**

But the blade doesn't find new skin.

It finds the old line. The one that's been there since a bright day and six lanes and a window that was already down — and the second steel touches that thirty-year scar, the scar *answers.* The room tips. The rain blows out white for half a second — the whole grey world overexposed to the blinding daylight of a bright afternoon. Somewhere far down and long ago a hundred horns start at once and don't stop —

*(— warm day, windows down, a woman driving, a man taking a glance wrong, the bird returning over the door —)*

— and then the man slams the door on it. Snaps back into the grey, into the rain, into the wet red running off his own elbow onto the floor of NOW. Breathing hard. Bleeding fresh over old.

**BRACE**

Because he understands the arrangement now, the real one, the one Arc couldn't warn him about because Arc is only a piece of him and this is bigger than any piece:

Every cut from here is going to find an old one.
Every blade in this book has a door in it.
And they only go *down.*

He picks the file up with the arm that still works. Leaves a red thumbprint on a nameless grey folder. At the edge of the frame the hand doesn't move — just waits, the way it waits, exactly where something was already going to swim.

*This is the shallow one,* the dark says, almost kind. *This is the one that barely hurt.*

**HOLD ON THE RED THUMBPRINT. FADE.**

---

### Note on placement
Cut 1 of the wound map — the shallowest, on purpose. It teaches the machine: a present-day blade finds a '96 scar, and the page drops. Here the drop is a half-second flash — the white-out, the horns, the glance — then it snaps back, so the reader learns to brace without being shown the floor yet. The case (the boy) enters fully veiled — recognized, never named. The devil's hand sits at the edge and does not reach, because it never has to. Arc's failure to gate it seeds the crew-as-mind reveal: the gatekeeper can't keep out the thing that came in wearing the man's own face.
